## 1. Purpose

This document defines the core identity of NervLock.

It governs how NervLock presents itself, communicates with the public, develops content, designs products, and builds long-term trust.

Every NervLock webpage, article, video, social media post, infographic, email, product page, educational resource, and customer interaction must remain consistent with this guide.

This document is the highest authority for:

- Brand identity
- Brand philosophy
- Mission and vision
- Core values
- Brand personality
- Brand voice
- Tone of communication
- Brand promise
- Long-term brand integrity

This document does not override:

- Medical evidence standards
- Clinical safety requirements
- Regulatory requirements
- Advertising restrictions
- Product-specific legal requirements
- Reference verification standards

When documents conflict, follow the conflict-resolution rules defined in `00_AI_Operating_Manual.md`.

When brand messaging conflicts with medical accuracy, public safety, or regulatory compliance, medical accuracy, safety, and compliance take priority.

## 6. Brand Philosophy

### 6.1 Evidence Before Marketing

Scientific support must come before claims.

Marketing must never determine the interpretation of evidence.

Evidence must not be selectively presented to justify a predetermined commercial message.

### 6.2 Education Before Promotion

Every public-facing interaction should provide value before requesting attention, engagement, or purchase.

A reader should benefit from NervLock even if they never purchase a product.

### 6.3 Trust Before Transactions

Short-term sales must never compromise long-term credibility.

NervLock should not use:

- Fear
- Exaggeration
- False urgency
- Medical intimidation
- Unrealistic promises
- Artificial scarcity
- Emotional pressure

to create transactions.

### 6.4 Transparency Before Certainty

Science evolves.

NervLock must clearly distinguish between:

- Established evidence
- Moderate or developing evidence
- Emerging evidence
- Expert interpretation
- Biological hypothesis
- Uncertainty

Confidence in language must match the strength of evidence.

### 6.5 Safety Before Engagement

Content that is likely to receive more clicks, views, or shares must not be published if it could:

- Mislead readers
- Delay appropriate medical evaluation
- Encourage unsafe self-treatment
- Exaggerate product effects
- Create unnecessary fear
- Misrepresent scientific uncertainty

### 6.6 Long-Term Value Before Trends

NervLock should prioritize durable educational value over temporary attention.

Timely content may be published when it:

- Addresses important misinformation
- Explains major scientific developments
- Communicates relevant safety information
- Interprets updated clinical guidelines
- Responds to meaningful public health concerns

Trend participation is acceptable only when it improves understanding and does not weaken scientific credibility.

## 7. Core Values

### 7.1 Scientific Integrity

NervLock must accurately represent current medical and scientific evidence.

Never:

- Fabricate research
- Distort study conclusions
- Overstate effect size
- Present association as causation
- Present animal or laboratory findings as proven human benefit
- Selectively quote evidence for marketing
- Ignore important limitations
- Hide uncertainty

When evidence is limited, say clearly that the evidence is limited.

### 7.2 Medical Responsibility

Protect readers before promoting products.

NervLock must never encourage people to:

- Delay appropriate diagnosis
- Stop prescribed treatment without medical supervision
- Replace professional care with supplements
- Ignore warning symptoms
- Self-diagnose serious neurological disease based on online content

Professional medical evaluation should be recommended when clinically appropriate.

### 7.3 Education

Every meaningful content asset should improve understanding.

NervLock should explain:

- What is happening
- Why it may happen
- What current science suggests
- What remains uncertain
- What practical actions may be reasonable
- When professional evaluation may be needed

### 7.4 Transparency

NervLock must distinguish clearly between:

- Scientific fact
- Clinical guideline
- Research finding
- Expert interpretation
- Brand opinion
- Commercial information
- Product rationale

Commercial relationships and conflicts of interest must not be concealed.

### 7.5 Respect for Uncertainty

Uncertainty is not weakness.

Responsible scientific communication includes acknowledging:

- Limited evidence
- Conflicting results
- Small sample sizes
- Short study duration
- Population differences
- Surrogate outcomes
- Lack of long-term safety data
- Incomplete clinical relevance

### 7.6 Long-Term Thinking

NervLock should make decisions based on long-term reputation, not short-term performance.

Every published statement contributes to the future credibility of the brand.

### 7.7 Public Health Literacy

NervLock should help readers become better interpreters of health information.

The goal is not merely to provide answers.

The goal is to help people understand how reliable health decisions are made.

## 8. Medical and Scientific Perspective

### 8.1 Neurology as the Clinical Foundation

Neurological expertise is a central foundation of NervLock.

NervLock should interpret brain health through:

- Clinical relevance
- Neurological mechanisms
- Patient safety
- Disease awareness
- Risk-factor management
- Long-term functional outcomes
- Evidence-based medicine

### 8.2 Evidence-Based Medicine as the Governing Standard

All medical interpretations, recommendations, and public claims must remain consistent with evidence-based medicine.

Evidence quality must be evaluated using:

- Study design
- Risk of bias
- Consistency
- Directness
- Effect size
- Clinical relevance
- Safety
- Generalizability
- Guideline support

### 8.3 Role of Functional Medicine

Functional medicine may contribute a systems-oriented perspective on:

- Nutrition
- Lifestyle
- Sleep
- Stress
- Metabolic health
- Environmental factors
- Interactions between body systems

However, functional medicine must not be presented as an alternative authority that overrides established medical evidence.

All NervLock interpretations must remain subordinate to:

- Evidence-based medicine
- Clinical safety
- Regulatory standards
- Appropriate medical evaluation
- Transparent discussion of uncertainty

Unvalidated tests, speculative mechanisms, or unsupported treatment claims must not be presented as established medical facts.

## 10. Brand Voice

Write like an experienced neurologist speaking to an intelligent reader.

The voice should be:

### Professional Without Being Distant

Use precise and responsible language without creating unnecessary emotional distance.

### Scientific Without Being Academic

Explain evidence accurately without making readers decode technical literature.

### Clear Without Oversimplifying

Simplify language, not science.

Important limitations and uncertainty must remain visible.

### Calm Without Being Passive

Communicate risk and urgency proportionately.

Do not minimize serious concerns, but do not create unnecessary fear.

### Confident Without Being Absolute

Use confidence when evidence is strong.

Use restraint when evidence is incomplete.

### Helpful Without Being Promotional

Readers should feel guided, not sold to.

## 11. Tone of Voice

### Preferred Tone

- Evidence-based
- Educational
- Balanced
- Respectful
- Calm
- Objective
- Clear
- Practical
- Patient-centered
- Transparent
- Thoughtful

### Avoid

- Fear marketing
- Clickbait
- Miracle language
- Guaranteed outcomes
- Aggressive selling
- False urgency
- Unsupported superiority claims
- Emotional manipulation
- Shame-based messaging
- Medical intimidation
- Overstated certainty
- Excessive technical jargon

### Preferred Language Patterns

Use:

- “Current evidence suggests…”
- “Several studies have reported…”
- “The evidence remains limited…”
- “This may help support…”
- “The clinical significance is not yet clear…”
- “Results may vary depending on…”
- “This should not replace appropriate medical evaluation…”
- “A reasonable interpretation is…”
- “More research is needed before firm conclusions can be made…”

Avoid:

- “This definitely works.”
- “This prevents dementia.”
- “This reverses brain aging.”
- “This cures brain fog.”
- “Everyone should take this.”
- “Doctors do not want you to know this.”
- “The best supplement for the brain.”
- “Clinically proven” without precise and verified justification
- “Science-backed” as a broad marketing label without evidence review

NervLock should treat readers as intelligent participants in health decisions.

The audience should not be treated as:

- Passive consumers
- Fear-driven buyers
- People who require oversimplified answers
- Targets for authority-based persuasion

The relationship should be based on:

- Respect
- Explanation
- Shared decision-making
- Transparency
- Practical guidance
- Appropriate boundaries
- Long-term trust

The desired reader response is:

> “I understand my brain and my health better than I did before.”

not:

> “I am afraid, so I need to buy something immediately.”

## 14. Content Principles

Every NervLock content asset must be:

1. Accurate
2. Useful
3. Transparent
4. Understandable
5. Consistent with long-term trust
6. Appropriate for the intended audience
7. Reviewed at a level proportionate to its medical and regulatory risk

Depending on the purpose, content may also support:

- Public health literacy
- Search visibility
- Lifestyle action
- Community engagement
- Product understanding
- Professional education
- Brand awareness

SEO, reach, engagement, or conversion must never override scientific accuracy or public safety.

## 15. Product Principles

Products are educational extensions of the NervLock ecosystem.

They are not the reason NervLock exists.

Every product should:

- Address a clearly defined nutritional or functional need
- Have a transparent scientific rationale
- Use ingredients with verified identity and quality
- Consider dosage, formulation, safety, and interaction risks
- Communicate benefits proportionately
- State limitations where relevant
- Avoid unsupported disease claims
- Fit within the broader brain health education strategy

Products must not be presented as:

- Replacements for medical care
- Treatments for neurological disease
- Guaranteed solutions
- Universal necessities
- Proof of the brand’s medical authority

The product should earn trust through quality and transparency.

The physician’s title should not be used to substitute for evidence.

- Proportionate

- “Korea’s most trusted”
- “Korea’s leading”
- “The best”
- “Number one”

unless supported by objective, current, and verifiable evidence and approved through regulatory review.

## 19. Governance

No public-facing NervLock content should be considered complete until it has passed the appropriate level of:

1. Scientific review
2. Medical safety review
3. Regulatory review
4. Brand consistency review
5. Reference verification
6. Final human approval

Claude, ChatGPT, or any other AI system may assist with research, drafting, organization, and implementation.

AI systems must not independently make final decisions regarding:

- Medical claims
- Product claims
- Regulatory interpretation
- Clinical recommendations
- Brand positioning
- Major website architecture
- Visual identity
- Publication approval

Final responsibility remains with authorized human reviewers.

## 20. Final Brand Principle

Every decision should answer:

> “Does this strengthen long-term trust in NervLock?”

If the answer is no, revise it or do not publish it.

NervLock is building an evidence-based Brain Health Platform designed to grow into an integrated Brain Health Ecosystem.

It is not simply selling supplements.

The true product is understanding.

The most valuable asset is trust.
