## 2. Governing Search Principle

NervLock follows one central search principle:

> Discoverability must be earned through useful, trustworthy, technically accessible, and clearly attributable information.

Search optimization must not determine medical truth.

Search volume must not determine evidence strength.

An AI system’s ability to extract a sentence does not justify oversimplification.

A high-ranking page that misleads readers is a strategic failure.

---

## 3. Strategic Role of SEO and GEO

SEO and GEO are distribution systems.

They are not:

- Evidence systems
- Medical review systems
- Regulatory approval systems
- Product-claim authorization systems
- Substitutes for original expertise
- Guarantees of indexing, ranking, citation, or traffic

Their role is to help the right audience find the right NervLock resource at the right stage of understanding.

NervLock should optimize for:

- Accurate discovery
- Topic comprehension
- Entity recognition
- Trust
- Citation
- Return visits
- Appropriate next actions

NervLock should not optimize solely for:

- Raw impressions
- Clickbait traffic
- Fear-driven queries
- Unqualified conversion
- Keyword density
- Content volume
- Artificial mentions

## 4. Definitions

### 4.1 Search Engine Optimization

SEO is the process of improving a website’s ability to be crawled, indexed, understood, and shown in traditional and AI-assisted search experiences.

### 4.2 Generative Engine Optimization

GEO is an internal NervLock term for improving the likelihood that reliable content is:

- Retrieved
- Understood
- Summarized accurately
- Cited
- Used as supporting material

by generative search and answer systems.

GEO is not a separate medical or editorial standard.

It must remain subordinate to ordinary SEO, content quality, evidence standards, and regulatory rules.

### 4.3 Answer Engine Optimization

AEO focuses on making information clear and useful for question-based search and direct answers.

### 4.4 AI Search Visibility

AI search visibility means that NervLock is:

- Mentioned
- Linked
- Cited
- Used as a supporting source
- Correctly represented

in an AI-assisted result.

### 4.5 Entity

An entity is a recognizable person, organization, product, medical concept, or topic.

Relevant NervLock entities include:

- NervLock
- NervLock Lab
- Authorized physician authors
- Brain health topics
- Future products
- Official social channels

### 4.6 Citation-Worthy Content

Content is citation-worthy when another publisher or answer system can confidently identify:

- Who created it
- What it says
- What evidence supports it
- When it was reviewed
- What limitations apply
- Where the original source can be found

## 10. Search Intent Classification

Every target query must be classified.

### Informational

The user wants to learn.

### Symptom Interpretation

The user wants to understand a symptom.

### Medical Navigation

The user wants to know whether or where to seek care.

### Comparative

The user wants to compare options, ingredients, tests, or approaches.

### Transactional

The user is considering a purchase or booking.

### Professional

The user seeks technical or clinical information.

### Emerging-News Intent

The user seeks current research, guideline, or safety information.

Mixed intent must be addressed according to the highest medical and regulatory risk.

## 11. Query Risk Classification

Search terms should be assessed independently from public wording.

### Green Query

Can be addressed directly and safely.

Example:

- 운동과 뇌 건강

### Yellow Query

Requires qualification or red-flag guidance.

Example:

- 기억력이 떨어지는 이유

### Orange Query

May imply diagnosis, treatment, or product efficacy.

Example:

- 브레인포그 영양제 추천

### Red Query

Contains a likely prohibited commercial claim.

Example:

- 치매 예방 영양제
- 파킨슨병 치료 영양제

Red queries may be used internally to understand user confusion.

They must not automatically become:

- Page titles
- Product names
- H1 headings
- Meta descriptions
- Ads
- CTAs

A safe educational page may explain why the searched assumption is inaccurate.

## 17. Content Gap Analysis

Content gaps should be classified as:

### Knowledge Gap

Important question not adequately explained.

### Interpretation Gap

Existing content reports facts but does not explain meaning.

### Safety Gap

Search results omit red flags or care boundaries.

### Evidence Gap

Existing content exaggerates preliminary research.

### Navigation Gap

Relevant content exists but cannot be found easily.

### Format Gap

The topic needs a table, guide, visual, video, or FAQ.

### Commercial Gap

Consumers need transparent product information.

Commercial gaps must not be prioritized ahead of safety and education gaps.

## 21. Titles

Titles must be:

- Accurate
- Specific
- Proportionate
- Useful
- Search-readable
- Consistent with the page

A title must not be stronger than the evidence.

### Preferred

- “브레인포그는 질병명일까? 가능한 원인과 진료가 필요한 경우”
- “수면과 기억력: 현재 근거는 무엇을 말할까?”
- “파킨슨병에서 변비가 흔한 이유와 관리 원칙”

### Avoid

- “치매가 시작됐다는 충격적인 신호”
- “뇌를 되살리는 최고의 영양제”
- “의사들이 숨기는 기억력 회복법”

The title may contain a searched misconception when the page clearly corrects it.

It must not reinforce the misconception as fact.

## 22. Title Tags

Every indexable page should have a unique title tag.

Title tags should:

- Identify the specific page
- Include the primary topic naturally
- Avoid keyword repetition
- Avoid unsupported claims
- Reflect language and audience
- Remain distinct from other pages

A practical internal format may be:

`[Specific Topic] | NervLock Lab`

Brand suffix use may vary by page and available display space.

Do not force the brand name into every title when it harms clarity.

---

## 23. Meta Descriptions

Meta descriptions should:

- Summarize the page accurately
- State the reader benefit
- Include relevant terminology naturally
- Avoid guarantees
- Avoid fear
- Avoid hidden product claims
- Differ across pages

Search engines may generate a different snippet.

The visible page must therefore contain the same accurate core answer.

---

## 24. Headings

Use:

- One clear H1
- Logical H2 sections
- H3 sections for meaningful detail

Headings should reflect real reader questions.

Do not use headings only to repeat keywords.

Do not hide a strong claim in a heading while qualifying it later in body text.

## 25. Answer-First Structure

For question-based pages, provide an accurate short answer near the top.

The answer should include:

- Bottom line
- Necessary qualification
- Key uncertainty
- Medical boundary where relevant

Example:

> “브레인포그는 공식 질환명이 아니라 집중력 저하, 피로감, 사고가 느린 느낌 등을 표현하는 일상적 용어입니다. 수면 부족이나 스트레스처럼 흔한 원인도 있지만, 증상이 지속되거나 신경학적 이상이 동반되면 평가가 필요합니다.”

Answer-first writing must not eliminate nuance from the full page.

## 36. URL Standards

URLs should be:

- Stable
- Descriptive
- Short enough to manage
- Lowercase where practical
- Free from unnecessary parameters
- Free from version numbers
- Consistent across languages

Avoid public canonical URLs such as:

- `/index_v3.html`
- `/final-new-2.html`
- `/blog-post-copy.html`

Use clean permanent URLs.

When a versioned development file becomes public, redirect it to the approved clean URL.

## 37. Canonicalization

Every indexable page should have one preferred URL.

Use:

- HTTP 301 redirect when the alternate URL should disappear
- `rel="canonical"` when valid duplicates must remain accessible

Do not canonicalize unrelated pages.

Canonical tags are signals, not substitutes for coherent architecture.

Align:

- Internal links
- Sitemap URLs
- Hreflang URLs
- Structured-data URLs
- Open Graph URLs
- Canonical URLs

## 42. Robots.txt

The robots.txt file must be located at the site root.

Example:

`https://nervlocklab.com/robots.txt`

A basic policy may include:

```text
User-agent: *
Allow: /

Disallow: /admin/
Disallow: /private/
Disallow: /staging/
Disallow: /internal-search/

Sitemap: https://nervlocklab.com/sitemap.xml
```

Rules must reflect the actual site.

Do not list private secrets in robots.txt.

Robots.txt controls crawling, not access security.

Use authentication for confidential content.

## 43. Search and AI Crawler Policy

Crawler policy must be an explicit business decision.

### Search Crawlers

Generally allow intended access for:

- Googlebot
- Naver Yeti
- Bingbot
- OAI-SearchBot

unless a page should not be public.

### OpenAI Crawlers

OpenAI separates major crawler functions.

- `OAI-SearchBot` is used for ChatGPT Search discovery and inclusion controls.
- `GPTBot` is used for content that may be used to improve generative AI foundation models.
- `ChatGPT-User` may access pages in response to user actions and is not the control used for Search inclusion.

NervLock may allow search discovery while making a separate decision about model-training access.

### Suggested OpenAI Policy — Search Allowed, Training Decision Separate

```text
User-agent: OAI-SearchBot
Allow: /

User-agent: GPTBot
Allow: /
```

or, when NervLock chooses to opt out of training while remaining discoverable in ChatGPT Search:

```text
User-agent: OAI-SearchBot
Allow: /

User-agent: GPTBot
Disallow: /
```

Crawler names and official documentation must be rechecked before deployment.

## 44. Indexability

An indexable page should normally:

- Return HTTP 200
- Be crawlable
- Have no `noindex`
- Have a valid canonical
- Contain meaningful visible content
- Be internally linked
- Appear in the sitemap when important

Use `noindex` for pages such as:

- Internal search results
- Thin filter pages
- Staging pages
- Duplicate utilities
- Private or account pages

Do not block a page in robots.txt when a crawler must see the `noindex` directive.

---

## 45. XML Sitemaps

Maintain an XML sitemap containing approved canonical indexable URLs.

The sitemap should:

- Use absolute HTTPS URLs
- Include only canonical URLs
- Exclude redirects
- Exclude errors
- Exclude noindex pages
- Use truthful `lastmod`
- Update when content materially changes
- Be submitted to relevant webmaster tools
- Be declared in robots.txt

Separate sitemaps may be used for:

- Articles
- Pages
- Products
- Images
- Videos
- Languages

A sitemap supports discovery.

It does not guarantee indexing or ranking.

## 46. IndexNow

NervLock should consider IndexNow for notifying participating search engines when approved URLs are:

- Created
- Materially updated
- Deleted
- Redirected

Implementation should:

- Use a valid key
- Automate approved URL submission
- Respect noindex status
- Avoid repeatedly submitting unchanged URLs
- Log submissions and responses

IndexNow can accelerate discovery notification.

It does not guarantee indexing.

---

## 47. HTTP Status Codes

Use meaningful status codes.

### 200

Valid page.

### 301 or 308

Permanent move.

### 302 or 307

Temporary move.

### 404

Page not found.

### 410

Intentionally removed.

### 5xx

Server error.

Do not return HTTP 200 for a missing page.

Do not redirect all missing URLs to the homepage.

---

## 48. Redirects

Redirects must be:

- Server-side where possible
- One-to-one
- Relevant
- Minimal in chain length
- Updated in internal links
- Reflected in sitemaps

During migration:

- Map old URL to best new equivalent
- Preserve language relationship
- Avoid redirect chains
- Monitor indexing
- Retain redirects for a sufficiently long operational period

Do not use JavaScript redirects as the primary migration method when server redirects are available.

---

## 49. JavaScript and Rendering

Important content should be present in a form that search systems can reliably access.

Prefer:

- Server-rendered HTML
- Static generation
- Progressive enhancement
- Meaningful HTML before interaction

Do not rely on user interaction to reveal:

- Main article content
- Author information
- References
- Product warnings
- Structured data
- Internal links

If JavaScript is used:

- Test rendered HTML
- Test canonical and metadata
- Test route status
- Test crawl access
- Test language alternates
- Test structured data

## 52. Core Web Vitals

At the review cutoff, current good-experience thresholds were:

- Largest Contentful Paint: 2.5 seconds or less
- Interaction to Next Paint: 200 milliseconds or less
- Cumulative Layout Shift: 0.1 or less

Evaluate field performance at the 75th percentile when data is available.

Treat thresholds as current operational references, not permanent rules.

Recheck official documentation during technical reviews.

- Guarantee AI citation
- Create evidence
- Authorize a claim
- Replace visible information

Use JSON-LD where practical.

Validate syntax and meaning.

- It does not conflict with visible content

- It does not replace sitemap, robots, HTML, or structured data

An experimental file must not become the primary content source.

- State when benefit is uncertain
- Avoid product recommendations in direct answers to disease queries

Snippet eligibility is secondary to safety.
