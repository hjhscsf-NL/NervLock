## 1. Purpose

This document defines the end-to-end workflow for planning, researching, drafting, reviewing, approving, publishing, updating, repurposing, correcting, and archiving NervLock blog content.

It applies to:

- NervLock Lab website articles
- Naver Blog posts
- Korean blog content
- English blog content
- Brain Health Academy articles
- Disease education
- Symptom education
- Lifestyle education
- Evidence reviews
- Research explainers
- Guideline updates
- Product-adjacent education
- Product science articles
- Professional education
- Caregiver education
- Patient education
- Guest contributions
- AI-assisted drafts
- Claude-generated drafts
- Claude Code implementation

The goal is not to publish the largest number of articles.

The goal is to build a durable, medically responsible, searchable, and trustworthy brain health knowledge system.

---

## 2. Governing Blog Principle

NervLock follows one central blog principle:

> Every article must begin with a real reader question and end with a clearer understanding, not a stronger sales impulse.

A blog article is not complete because:

- The text is fluent
- The keyword is present
- The word count is long
- The references are numerous
- The design is attractive
- The article has been uploaded

An article is complete only after the appropriate scientific, medical, regulatory, reference, brand, SEO/GEO, UX, and human-review steps have been documented.

---

## 3. Strategic Role of the Blog

The NervLock blog serves five strategic functions.

### 3.1 Education

Explain brain health, neurological conditions, symptoms, lifestyle factors, and scientific evidence clearly.

### 3.2 Trust

Demonstrate consistent medical responsibility, transparency, and respect for uncertainty.

### 3.3 Search Authority

Build durable topic clusters that search engines and AI systems can discover, interpret, and cite.

### 3.4 Community Development

Answer meaningful questions from readers, patients, caregivers, and health-conscious adults.

### 3.5 Responsible Product Context

Explain the evidence, safety, and limitations relevant to a product category only when the product has a legitimate educational role.

The blog must not become a disguised sales catalog.

---

## 4. Blog Success Definition

A successful NervLock article should achieve one or more of the following:

- Improve reader understanding
- Correct misinformation
- Clarify when medical evaluation is needed
- Explain evidence quality
- Support a durable topic cluster
- Earn qualified search visibility
- Become a reliable source for AI citation
- Generate thoughtful reader questions
- Support appropriate internal navigation
- Reduce product misunderstanding
- Strengthen long-term brand trust

Traffic, ranking, saves, shares, subscriptions, and conversion are secondary outcome measures.

They do not override accuracy or safety.

## 5. Article Types

Every proposed article must be classified before research begins.

### 5.1 Foundational Pillar Article

Broad and durable overview of a major topic.

Examples:

- Brain Health
- Sleep and Brain Health
- Healthy Cognitive Aging
- Parkinson’s Disease
- Dementia and Cognitive Decline

### 5.2 Cluster Article

Focused article answering one defined question.

Examples:

- 수면 부족은 기억력에 어떤 영향을 줄까?
- 파킨슨병에서 변비가 흔한 이유
- 운동은 인지기능에 어떤 영향을 줄까?

### 5.3 Symptom Guide

Explains a symptom, possible causes, red flags, and care boundaries.

### 5.4 Disease Guide

Explains a neurological condition, diagnosis, treatment, prognosis, and caregiver considerations.

### 5.5 Evidence Review

Evaluates a focused research question using structured evidence appraisal.

### 5.6 Research Explainer

Explains a new study or research development without overstating preliminary findings.

### 5.7 Guideline Update

Summarizes and interprets a new or revised clinical guideline.

### 5.8 Lifestyle Education

Covers sleep, exercise, nutrition, stress, metabolic health, and sustainable behavior.

### 5.9 Myth or Misconception Correction

Addresses a common misleading claim.

### 5.10 Caregiver Guide

Supports families and caregivers with practical, medically responsible information.

### 5.11 Product-Adjacent Education

Discusses a nutritional or functional topic connected to a future product area without converting the article into a product advertisement.

### 5.12 Product Science Article

Explains a specific NervLock product’s rationale, evidence, safety, dose, limitations, and role.

### 5.13 Professional Education

Written for physicians or healthcare professionals.

### 5.14 Brand Education

Explains NervLock’s mission, evidence standards, or editorial approach.

### 5.15 Correction or Safety Notice

Corrects a material error or communicates an important safety update.

Mixed articles must follow the highest-risk applicable workflow.

## 7. Canonical Content Principle

The strategic home of each article must be defined.

For durable medical knowledge, the preferred canonical home is usually the NervLock Lab website.

When similar material appears on Naver Blog or another platform:

- Do not create uncontrolled full-text duplication
- Adjust the framing, depth, structure, and CTA
- Preserve the same medical meaning
- Link naturally to the fuller source where appropriate
- Record which version is authoritative
- Update all material versions when evidence or safety information changes

An article’s canonical home is an editorial decision, not merely a technical tag.

---

## 8. Korean and English Content Principle

Korean and English articles are separate editorial assets.

They may be:

- Equivalent localized versions
- Related but independently written articles
- Original articles serving different search intent

Do not assume that:

- Korean content must always be translated into English
- English content must always be replaced by a Korean translation
- Equivalent words have equivalent search intent
- The same regulatory wording applies in both languages

Each version requires separate review for:

- Audience
- Search intent
- Medical terminology
- Evidence wording
- Regulation
- CTA
- References
- Internal links
- Metadata

## 11. Topic Selection Criteria

A topic may proceed when it has:

- Clear reader value
- Strategic fit
- Sufficient evidence
- Reasonable update capacity
- Search or community relevance
- Distinct contribution
- Appropriate medical boundaries
- Acceptable regulatory risk

A topic should be rejected or deferred when:

- It depends on sensational framing
- It requires an unsupported product claim
- It duplicates existing content without added value
- Evidence is too weak for the intended conclusion
- The topic is outside NervLock’s competence
- The topic could encourage unsafe self-diagnosis
- The article would be commercially motivated but disguised as neutral education
- The team cannot maintain the content safely

---

## 12. Editorial Priority Model

Topics should be prioritized in this order:

1. Urgent safety and misinformation correction
2. Foundational brain health education
3. High-value symptom and disease education
4. Caregiver needs
5. Evidence-based lifestyle education
6. Strategic topic clusters
7. Guideline and evidence updates
8. Product-adjacent education
9. Product science
10. Trend-driven content

Commercial opportunity must not move a low-value topic above a high-priority safety or education need.

## 13. Stage 2 — Strategic Fit Review

Before research begins, answer:

- Does the topic belong within brain health?
- Does it reinforce NervLock’s evidence-based platform identity?
- Does it educate before promoting?
- Does it fit an approved topic cluster?
- Is the article useful without a product mention?
- Does it strengthen long-term trust?
- Does NervLock have appropriate expertise?
- Could the topic create brand confusion?

Possible decisions:

- Proceed
- Proceed With Scope Adjustment
- Reframe
- Assign to Another Channel
- Hold
- Reject

## 17. Stage 6 — Search and Content-Gap Review

Search analysis should determine:

- What users are actually asking
- What types of results appear
- What information is missing
- What misinformation is common
- Whether the query implies diagnosis or treatment
- Whether current results lack red flags
- Whether existing NervLock content already answers the question
- Which page type is appropriate
- Whether the article should be canonical on the website or adapted for Naver

Do not create a topic only because a keyword has high volume.

## 19. Stage 7 — Evidence Question Definition

Before searching, define the question.

Use:

- PICO for interventions
- PECO for exposures and risk factors
- Diagnostic framework for tests
- Prognostic framework for future outcomes
- Mechanistic framework for pathways
- Safety framework for harms and interactions

Record:

- Population
- Exposure or intervention
- Comparator
- Outcome
- Time frame
- Setting
- Intended public conclusion

Do not search broadly and decide the question after finding favorable evidence.

---

## 20. Stage 8 — Evidence Search

For medical articles, prioritize:

1. Current clinical guidelines
2. Systematic reviews
3. Pivotal randomized trials
4. Major observational studies
5. Safety sources
6. Primary mechanistic research where relevant
7. Official public health or regulatory sources

Search depth must match article risk.

For high-risk articles:

- Search more than one reliable source or database when feasible
- Include contradictory evidence
- Check recent updates
- Check retractions and corrections
- Record the search date
- Record the evidence cutoff

A casual web search is not sufficient for a high-risk article.

---

- Safety evidence
- Retraction checks

## 26. Stage 12 — Outline Approval

The outline should establish the argument before prose is written.

A typical outline may include:

1. Title
2. Summary
3. Why the topic matters
4. Definition
5. Causes or mechanisms
6. Current evidence
7. Uncertainty
8. Practical actions
9. Red flags
10. Product role, only if relevant
11. Key takeaways
12. References
13. Review information

The outline must be reviewed for:

- Logic
- Completeness
- Evidence placement
- Safety
- Product separation
- Search intent
- Internal links

A weak outline should be corrected before drafting.

## 27. Stage 13 — Drafting

Draft according to `07_Content_Writing_Guide.md`.

The draft must:

- Answer the reader’s primary question
- Use evidence-calibrated language
- Separate mechanism from outcome
- Separate association from causation
- State uncertainty
- Include practical guidance
- Include red flags where relevant
- Avoid unsupported product integration
- Use clear headings
- Preserve citation placement
- Use a calm, professional voice

AI-generated text remains an internal draft.

---

## 28. Article Length

Length should follow the question, not a fixed SEO target.

A page should be long enough to:

- Answer the question
- Explain evidence
- State limitations
- Address safety
- Support navigation

It should not be lengthened through:

- Repetition
- Generic definitions
- Keyword stuffing
- Unnecessary history
- Redundant FAQs
- Inflated introductions
- AI-generated filler

Concise completeness is preferable to artificial length.

---

## 29. Stage 14 — Scientific Review

The scientific reviewer should assess:

- Accuracy
- Evidence selection
- Evidence certainty
- Study interpretation
- Effect size
- Absolute versus relative effects
- Clinical significance
- Applicability
- Mechanism
- Contradictory evidence
- Limitations
- Safety

Possible decisions:

- Usable
- Usable With Qualification
- Revision Required
- Insufficient Evidence
- Not Recommended
- Specialist Review Required

Scientific review does not authorize a commercial claim.

---

## 30. Stage 15 — Medical Safety Review

The medical reviewer should assess:

- Could readers delay care?
- Could readers self-diagnose?
- Are important differential diagnoses omitted?
- Are red flags adequate?
- Is self-care advice safe?
- Are medication issues addressed?
- Are vulnerable populations considered?
- Is the tone proportionate?
- Are urgent symptoms clearly separated from routine concerns?

Medical review is mandatory for symptom, disease, treatment, and product-related articles.

---

## 35. Title Review

The final title should be:

- Accurate
- Specific
- Proportionate
- Search-readable
- Non-sensational
- Consistent with the body

The title may contain a misconception only when the article clearly corrects it.

The title must not imply:

- Diagnosis
- Guaranteed prevention
- Treatment
- Product efficacy
- Certainty beyond the evidence

---

## 36. Summary Review

The summary should:

- State the bottom line
- Include essential qualification
- State uncertainty
- Avoid product promotion
- Remain accurate if extracted
- Be understandable without the full article

The summary must not be stronger than the body.

---

## 37. Internal-Link Review

Every article should link to:

- Relevant pillar
- Closely related cluster content
- Definitions where useful
- Red-flag or care guidance
- Related evidence review
- Relevant product science only when appropriate

Do not:

- Link every disease article to a product
- Use vague anchor text
- Add irrelevant link blocks
- Create hidden product funnels

---

## 38. Product Integration Decision

Before adding a product or product category, confirm:

1. Is the article complete without the product?
2. Is the product relevant to the exact question?
3. Is the transition educationally natural?
4. Is the evidence sufficient?
5. Is the wording legally permitted?
6. Is the commercial relationship clear?
7. Could the reader mistake the content for treatment advice?
8. Does the article remain valuable to a non-buyer?

If any answer is unresolved, remove the product reference or route for further review.

---

## 39. CTA Review

- Follow NervLock

### Product CTA

Use only in appropriate commercial context and after regulatory review.

Do not use:

- Fear
- False urgency
- Disease pressure
- Caregiver guilt
- Guaranteed outcome
- Artificial scarcity

6. Avoid identical full-text duplication
7. Link to the canonical long-form article when useful
8. Use platform-appropriate images

## 58. Correction Workflow

When an error is identified:

1. Preserve the original version
2. Assess medical significance
3. Assess regulatory significance
4. Stop paid or scheduled distribution if needed
5. Correct the source article
6. Correct derivative content
7. Update references
8. Record the change
9. Add a correction note when material
10. Notify reviewers
11. Re-submit for indexing where appropriate

Material safety or factual errors should not be silently corrected.

## 59. Content Consolidation

Consolidate articles when:

- They answer the same intent
- They compete for the same query
- One is thin
- Evidence has been fragmented
- The distinction does not help readers
- Updating several versions creates risk

Choose the strongest canonical URL.

Preserve:

- Useful sections
- References
- Internal links
- Redirects
- Historical records

---

## 60. Archiving

Archive an article when:

- It is obsolete
- It is superseded
- Evidence is no longer sufficient
- The topic no longer fits strategy
- The article cannot be maintained safely
- The article creates unresolved misunderstanding
- The content duplicates a stronger article

Possible actions:

- Redirect
- Keep with archive notice
- Remove with HTTP 410
- Convert to historical note

The action must be chosen according to reader value, search impact, and safety.

## 71. Blog Quality Standard

A NervLock blog article is publication-ready only when it is:

- Scientifically accurate
- Medically responsible
- Regulatorily defensible
- Properly referenced
- Brand-consistent
- Reader-centered
- Search-accessible
- Technically sound
- Updateable
- Human-approved

Fluency is not approval.

Traffic is not evidence.

Ranking is not trust.

A physician title is not proof.

A disclaimer is not a repair tool.

A product mention is not a required ending.

A short-form title must not be stronger than the article.

A translated version must not be treated as automatically equivalent.

A live page is not final if it has not passed review.

## 72. Final Blog Principle

Every NervLock article should answer four questions:

1. What should the reader understand?
2. How strong is the evidence?
3. What remains uncertain?
4. What is a reasonable next step?

When the article concerns symptoms or disease, it must also answer:

5. When is medical evaluation needed?

When the article concerns a product, it must also answer:

6. What can the product reasonably do?
7. What can it not be claimed to do?
8. Who should use caution?

The blog should become a reliable public knowledge system, not a content factory.

NervLock should publish less when necessary, update more when needed, and protect trust at every stage.
