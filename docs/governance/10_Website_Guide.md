## 1. Purpose

This document defines how the NervLock website must be planned, structured, written, designed, implemented, reviewed, deployed, maintained, and expanded.

It governs:

- Website strategy
- Information architecture
- Homepage structure
- Brain Health Academy structure
- Blog structure
- Topic hubs and content clusters
- Product and product-science areas
- Author and medical-review pages
- Korean and English website architecture
- Navigation
- User journeys
- Search and AI discoverability
- Trust signals
- Medical and regulatory separation
- Accessibility
- Mobile experience
- Technical implementation
- Performance
- Security
- Privacy
- Analytics
- Forms
- Version control
- Deployment
- Maintenance
- Claude Code instructions
- Final website quality assurance

The website must function first as a brain health knowledge platform.

It must not function primarily as a product catalog.

---

## 2. Governing Website Principle

NervLock follows one central website principle:

> The website must guide visitors from brain health questions to education, from education to science, from science to trust, and only then, when appropriate, to products.

The required journey is:

**Brain Health**  
↓  
**Education**  
↓  
**Science**  
↓  
**Trust**  
↓  
**Products**

This order must not be reversed.

The homepage, global navigation, visual hierarchy, internal links, CTAs, and product placement must reinforce this sequence.

---

## 3. Strategic Role of the Website

The website is the primary owned digital property of NervLock.

It should serve as:

- The canonical home of NervLock’s brand identity
- The primary repository for durable brain health education
- The central platform for evidence interpretation
- The source of truth for NervLock product science
- The canonical home for medically reviewed articles
- The central destination linked from social media and video
- The primary platform for search and AI discovery
- The controlled environment for updates and corrections
- The long-term foundation of the NervLock ecosystem

The website should not depend on any single social platform for authority, traffic, or business continuity.

---

## 4. Website Success Definition

The website is successful when it:

- Helps visitors understand brain health more clearly
- Makes medical and scientific information easier to evaluate
- Clearly separates education from commerce
- Supports safe medical navigation
- Builds durable topic authority
- Is easy to search, navigate, and revisit
- Preserves evidence, references, and review history
- Provides a credible foundation for future products
- Converts only after trust and understanding are established
- Maintains long-term technical and editorial integrity

Traffic, product clicks, and sales are secondary measures.

A website that converts well but weakens medical trust is a strategic failure.

---

## 5. Official Website Positioning

The website should communicate NervLock as:

**A neurologist-led, evidence-based brain health education and science communication platform with evidence-informed products.**

The website must not create the primary impression that NervLock is:

- A supplement store
- A discount marketplace
- A clinic advertisement
- A disease-treatment seller
- A generic wellness blog
- A functional-medicine claims site
- A biohacking platform
- An anonymous AI-content site

The first visible screen and global navigation should reinforce education and expertise before commerce.

### 6.2 Symptom Searchers

- Safety information
- Questions to discuss with clinicians

### 6.4 Evidence-Seeking Consumers

Needs:

- Study interpretation
- Ingredient evidence
- Dose and safety context
- Transparent uncertainty
- Product limitations

### 6.5 Healthcare Professionals

Needs:

- More technical evidence summaries
- Guideline interpretation
- Reference traceability

### 6.6 Prospective Product Customers

Needs:

- Clear product identity
- Permitted benefit territory
- Ingredient rationale
- Safety
- Intended population
- Limitations
- Commercial transparency

Do not force one page to serve all audiences equally.

## 7. Core User Journeys

The website should support at least the following journeys.

### Journey A — General Brain Health

Homepage  
→ Brain Health Hub  
→ Foundational guide  
→ Related cluster article  
→ Newsletter or follow option

### Journey B — Symptom Concern

Search result  
→ Symptom guide  
→ Red-flag section  
→ Related disease or lifestyle guide  
→ Appropriate medical-navigation CTA

### Journey C — Disease Education

Disease hub  
→ Foundational disease article  
→ Symptoms, diagnosis, treatment, caregiver, lifestyle, FAQ  
→ Related evidence review

### Journey D — Evidence Evaluation

Research or ingredient page  
→ Evidence summary  
→ Limitations  
→ References  
→ Related product science, only when appropriate

### Journey E — Product Evaluation

Product page  
→ What it is  
→ Who it is for  
→ Ingredients and dose  
→ Evidence and limitations  
→ Safety and exclusions  
→ Purchase decision

### Journey F — Brand Trust

About NervLock  
→ Editorial standards  
→ Author or reviewer profile  
→ Evidence and reference policies  
→ Educational content

Every journey must be useful without requiring purchase.

## 9. Homepage Role

The homepage is not a content dump.

It should answer:

1. What is NervLock?
2. Who is it for?
3. What can visitors learn here?
4. Why should they trust the information?
5. Where should they go next?
6. How do products fit into the ecosystem?

The homepage must communicate the platform before the product.

## 10. Homepage Content Hierarchy

Recommended order:

### Section 1 — Hero

Purpose:

- Define NervLock
- State the value proposition
- Provide one primary educational CTA

Preferred message territory:

- Evidence-based brain health
- Neurologist-led interpretation
- Clear explanations
- Long-term brain health

Preferred CTA:

- Explore Brain Health
- Start With the Academy
- Read the Latest Evidence

Avoid:

- Shop Now as the primary CTA
- Disease fear
- Product hero imagery
- Discount language
- Unsupported superiority

### Section 2 — Core Brain Health Areas

Introduce major topic pathways such as:

- Memory and Cognitive Aging
- Sleep
- Exercise
- Nutrition
- Stress and Autonomic Function
- Metabolic Brain Health
- Pain and the Nervous System
- Parkinson’s Disease
- Dementia Education

### Section 3 — How NervLock Interprets Science

Explain:

- Evidence quality
- Clinical relevance
- Uncertainty
- Safety
- Why mechanisms are not the same as proven benefit

### Section 4 — Brain Health Academy

Feature:

- Foundational guides
- Learning pathways
- Topic hubs
- Evidence explainers

### Section 5 — Latest or Featured Articles

Display selected content based on quality and strategic relevance.

Do not show only the newest content.

### Section 6 — Meet the Medical Leadership

Show:

- Verified physician identity
- Relevant specialty
- Editorial role
- Link to full profile
- Conflict-of-interest transparency

### Section 7 — Product Science and Products

Products may appear only after education and trust sections.

The product section should explain:

- Why products exist
- How they are evaluated
- What they can and cannot claim
- Link to product science before or alongside purchase

### Section 8 — Trust and Editorial Standards

Link to:

- Medical evidence standard
- Reference policy
- Regulatory principles
- Corrections policy
- Editorial process

### Section 9 — Newsletter or Community CTA

Use a calm educational invitation.

Avoid urgency or fear.

### Section 10 — Footer

Include:

- Navigation
- Language
- Contact
- Privacy
- Terms
- Medical disclaimer
- Editorial standards
- Product information
- Social links
- Copyright

- Organize content by level
- Provide topic pathways
- Explain evidence interpretation
- Support repeated learning
- Connect related topics
- Build long-term trust

#### Level 3 — Clinical Education

- Parkinson’s Disease

## 16. Product Science Section

Product science should be separated from general disease education.

Recommended structure:

- Product development philosophy
- Ingredient selection criteria
- Dose rationale
- Safety and interactions
- Quality standards
- Finished-product evidence
- Limitations
- Conflicts of interest
- Related references

Product science pages should not use disease-treatment positioning.

A product science page is not permission to exceed the approved communication territory.

---

## 17. Products Section

The products section must remain subordinate to the education platform.

Each product category page should explain:

- The consumer need
- The category
- The role of lifestyle
- The limits of products
- Who may consider the product
- Who should use caution
- The difference between ingredient evidence and finished-product evidence

Each product page should link to:

- Product science
- Safety
- FAQ
- Ingredient details
- Quality information
- Relevant educational content

Disease pages should not be placed under product categories.

---

1. Product name
2. Product category
3. Short neutral description

5. Who it is for
6. Who should use caution

9. What current evidence suggests
10. What remains uncertain
11. How to use

14. FAQ
15. References

## 19. Product Page Prohibitions

Do not use:

- Disease prevention
- Disease treatment
- Cure
- Reversal
- Guaranteed memory improvement
- Guaranteed sleep improvement
- Doctor guarantee
- “Clinically proven” without approved substantiation
- Unsupported superiority
- False ratings
- Manipulated testimonials
- Brain repair visuals
- Medicine-like imagery

Do not use a small disclaimer to repair a strong main claim.

## 24. Search Function

Website search should:

- Return relevant content
- Support Korean and English
- Distinguish articles, pages, and products
- Avoid prioritizing products over education by default
- Handle synonyms
- Handle common misspellings where practical
- Display no-result guidance
- Avoid exposing private content

Search results should identify:

- Content type
- Title
- Summary
- Date
- Author
- Language

Internal search-result pages should generally not be indexed unless a deliberate strategy exists.

## 25. Global Navigation

Navigation must be:

- Stable
- Predictable
- Mobile-accessible
- Keyboard-accessible
- Limited in depth
- Consistent across languages
- Consistent across page types

The main navigation should not change substantially from page to page.

Do not place every topic in the top navigation.

Use hubs, mega menus, or structured topic pages when needed.

## 36. Current Homepage Transition Standard

The approved Korean homepage must become:

`https://nervlocklab.com/`

The approved English homepage must become:

`https://nervlocklab.com/en/`

Implementation must preserve:

- Existing Korean blog article URLs
- Existing English blog article URLs
- Existing original English content
- Existing internal links where possible
- Search value of established pages
- Existing metadata and references unless revised

The Korean homepage replacement must not delete or overwrite existing blog articles.

The English homepage must not automatically convert all English content into translations of Korean content.

Existing original English articles remain independent editorial assets unless a separate migration decision is approved.

## 37. Homepage File Migration

When a development homepage such as `index_v3.html` is approved:

1. Create backup
2. Confirm final content
3. Confirm all asset paths
4. Move approved page to root homepage behavior
5. Preserve clean canonical
6. Redirect obsolete versioned URL where publicly accessible
7. Update internal links
8. Update sitemap
9. Test Korean and English routes
10. Verify no blog content was overwritten

Claude Code must provide a file-level change report and rollback instructions.

## 38. Multilingual Architecture

Korean is the root-language site unless strategy changes.

English should live under `/en/`.

Each language page must include:

- Correct `lang` attribute
- Language-specific title
- Language-specific description
- Self-referencing canonical
- Hreflang when equivalent
- Human-reviewed copy
- Appropriate terminology
- Jurisdiction-appropriate claims
- Language-specific navigation

Do not automatically generate an English version for every Korean article.

Do not use hreflang for pages that are not meaningfully equivalent.

---

## 39. Language Switcher

The language switcher should:

- Be visible but not dominant
- Link to equivalent page when available
- Link to language homepage when no equivalent exists
- Preserve user context when possible
- Avoid automatic geolocation redirects
- Avoid forcing browser language

Label clearly:

- 한국어
- English

Do not use flags as the only language indicator.

---

## 40. Canonical and Hreflang Rules

Each indexable page should have:

- One canonical URL
- Self-referencing canonical
- Reciprocal hreflang when equivalents exist

Possible hreflang:

- `ko-KR`
- `en`
- `x-default` where appropriate

Do not:

- Canonicalize Korean to English
- Canonicalize English to Korean
- Use hreflang for unrelated pages
- Point hreflang to redirects
- Include noindex pages in hreflang sets

Internal links should support:

- Topic hierarchy

- Related evidence
- Medical navigation
- Product transparency
- Language relationships

Every pillar should link to important clusters.

Every disease page should link to related symptoms, treatment education, caregiver content, and relevant evidence.

Product links should appear only when contextually justified.

## 42. Education and Commerce Separation

The website must visibly distinguish:

- Education
- Product science
- Product information
- Sales

Required separation controls:

- Distinct page labels
- Distinct navigation areas
- Transparent commercial CTA
- No hidden affiliate behavior
- No product CTA in urgent medical warnings
- No automatic product recommendation at the end of disease pages
- Clear conflict disclosure
- Separate review workflow

A product page may link to education.

A disease page must not become a hidden product funnel.

## 44. Trust Signals

Trust signals should include:

- Verified author identity
- Medical reviewer identity
- Publication date
- Review date
- Evidence cutoff
- References
- Correction history
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure
- Editorial standards
- Product separation
- Contact information

Avoid fake trust signals such as:

- Unverified awards
- Unsupported “No. 1”

- False institutional affiliation

---

## 45. Medical Disclaimer

A medical disclaimer should clarify that content is:

- General education
- Not individualized diagnosis
- Not individualized treatment
- Not a substitute for professional care

The disclaimer must not be used to justify misleading content.

For urgent symptoms, the page should provide specific care guidance rather than relying only on a general footer disclaimer.

---

## 46. Commercial Disclosure

When NervLock has a commercial interest, disclose it clearly.

Possible disclosures include:

- NervLock develops or sells the discussed product
- The author has a financial interest
- A partner relationship exists
- A link is affiliate or sponsored
- A sample was provided

The disclosure should be proximate to the relevant content.

Do not hide it only in the footer.

---

## 47. Testimonials and Reviews

Product reviews must be:

- Genuine
- Verifiable
- Unmanipulated
- Appropriately disclosed
- Legally usable
- Not used to make prohibited claims

Do not use:

- AI-generated reviews presented as real
- Disease-treatment testimonials
- Edited reviews that intensify efficacy
- False review counts
- Selective reviews that create a misleading typical result

## 99. Final Website Principles

The website is not a supplement catalog.

The homepage is not a sales page.

The Brain Health Academy is not a content archive without structure.

A disease page is not a product funnel.

A physician title is not a substitute for evidence.

A disclaimer is not permission to mislead.

A fast website is not successful if the content is unsafe.

A beautiful page is not complete if it lacks review and references.

An English page is not automatically a translation of a Korean page.

A live page is not final if it has not passed approval.

A product should appear only after the website has established education, science, and trust.

The NervLock website should become the most reliable place within the NervLock ecosystem to understand what is known, what remains uncertain, and what a reasonable next step may be.
