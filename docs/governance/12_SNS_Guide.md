## 1. Purpose

This document defines how NervLock must plan, create, review, approve, publish, distribute, moderate, measure, revise, and archive social media content.

It governs:

- Instagram
- Facebook
- Threads
- Instagram Stories
- Instagram Reels
- Instagram carousels
- Facebook posts and Reels
- Short-form cross-distribution
- Social captions
- Hashtags
- Highlights
- Pinned posts
- Community interaction
- Comments
- Direct messages
- Social listening
- Influencer and partner content
- User-generated content
- Paid social promotion
- Product-related social content
- Medical education
- Disease and symptom education
- Research communication
- Crisis communication
- Corrections
- Analytics
- Account security
- AI-assisted content creation
- Content adaptation from the NervLock website and blog

The purpose of social media is not to maximize posting volume.

The purpose is to make accurate brain health education discoverable, understandable, useful, and memorable while building long-term trust.

---

## 2. Governing Social Media Principle

NervLock follows one central social media rule:

> Every post must leave the audience better informed, not more frightened or more vulnerable to marketing.

Social media may simplify format.

It must not simplify away:

- Evidence uncertainty
- Medical boundaries
- Important red flags
- Material safety information
- Commercial disclosure
- Product limitations

A short post is not exempt from scientific or regulatory standards.

A viral format is not permission to overstate evidence.

---

## 3. Strategic Role of Social Media

Social media serves six strategic functions.

### 3.1 Discovery

Introduce NervLock to people who have not yet visited the website.

### 3.2 Education

Explain one useful brain health concept at a time.

### 3.3 Trust Formation

Demonstrate consistent medical responsibility, evidence interpretation, and calm communication.

### 3.4 Community Development

Create a respectful space for questions, discussion, and repeated learning.

### 3.5 Content Navigation

Guide users toward:

- NervLock Lab
- Brain Health Academy
- Relevant articles
- Evidence reviews
- Longer video content
- Appropriate medical care when needed

### 3.6 Responsible Product Context

Explain a product’s role, evidence, limitations, and safety only after the educational foundation and regulatory pathway are established.

Social media is a distribution and relationship channel.

It is not the canonical source for complex medical evidence.

---

## 4. Social Media Positioning

NervLock social channels should communicate:

**A neurologist-led, evidence-based brain health education platform that explains complex science clearly and responsibly.**

The channels should not primarily resemble:

- A supplement storefront
- A clinic advertisement
- A personal influencer account
- A fear-based neurological disease account
- A generic wellness page
- A motivational quote page
- A content farm
- A medical diagnosis service
- A product discount channel

The desired audience response is:

> “I understand this topic more clearly, and I know where to learn more.”

not:

> “I am afraid and need to buy something immediately.”

---

## 5. Relationship to the Website

The NervLock website is the preferred canonical home for durable medical knowledge.

Social content should:

- Introduce the question
- Explain the central idea
- State the practical meaning
- Identify uncertainty
- Direct readers to deeper information when needed

Social media should not attempt to replace:

- Full evidence reviews
- Complete reference lists
- Detailed safety discussions
- Long-form clinical education
- Product science documentation

When a social post derives from a website article, record the canonical source.

When the source article is materially updated, derivative social content must be reviewed.

## 8. Current Platform Format Baseline

Platform capabilities change.

The following are operational baselines verified at this document’s review cutoff.

### Instagram Reels

Instagram supports multiple Reel aspect ratios, including vertical 9:16.

NervLock standard:

- 1080 × 1920
- 9:16
- Central safe zone
- Reviewed subtitles
- Reviewed cover

### Instagram Feed Images

Instagram supports images at a width of at least 1080 pixels across a range of aspect ratios.

NervLock preferred feed formats:

- 1080 × 1350 for educational portrait content
- 1080 × 1080 when square is strategically appropriate

### Meta Stories and Reels

Meta recommends vertical 9:16 for full-screen Stories and Reels placements.

NervLock standard:

- 1080 × 1920
- 9:16
- Important content away from interface edges

### YouTube Shorts

YouTube currently accepts square or vertical Shorts up to three minutes.

NervLock may use shorter internal production targets depending on the message.

These platform specifications must be rechecked before:

- Paid campaigns
- Major template revisions
- New channel launches
- Automated publishing changes

## 9. Content Success Definition

A successful social post should achieve one or more of the following:

- Improve understanding
- Correct misinformation
- Clarify uncertainty
- Identify a reasonable next step
- Clarify when care may be needed
- Encourage high-quality saving or sharing
- Guide users to a trusted NervLock resource
- Generate meaningful questions
- Strengthen brand recognition
- Support a durable topic cluster

Views and follower count are secondary.

A high-view post that creates medical misunderstanding is a failure.

## 13. Content Mix

A recommended strategic mix is:

- 60–75% foundational education and practical brain health
- 15–25% symptom, disease, caregiver, and medical navigation
- 10–15% evidence interpretation and brand trust
- 0–10% product science and commercial content before product launch
- Product content may increase after launch only when education remains clearly dominant

These ranges are planning guides, not rigid quotas.

`W02_Content_Calendar.md` governs the active schedule.

### Instagram

- Feed or carousel: 2–4 per week

- Stories: 4–7 days per week
- Live or Q&A: only when a defined topic and moderation plan exist

### Facebook

- 2–4 adapted posts per week
- Selected Reels
- Article distribution
- Caregiver-focused discussion

### Threads

- 3–7 concise posts per week
- Replies and discussion based on available moderation capacity

These are internal starting ranges.

They are not platform algorithm requirements.

Quality and response capacity take priority over volume.

## 17. Carousel Structure

A typical educational carousel may use:

1. Cover
2. Why the topic matters
3. Definition
4. Main explanation
5. Evidence
6. Practical meaning
7. Red flags or limitations
8. Key takeaway
9. CTA

The number of slides must follow the topic.

Do not add slides merely to increase swipe count.

### Cover Requirements

- Short title
- One visual concept
- One clear promise of educational value
- No unsupported outcome claim
- Small brand identifier

---

## 18. Four-Story Sequence

A standard four-story sequence may use:

### Story 1 — Question or Hook

Example:

> “기억력이 떨어진 느낌, 모두 치매의 시작일까요?”

### Story 2 — Core Explanation

Explain the main distinction clearly.

### Story 3 — Practical Meaning or Red Flag

State what may be reasonable and when evaluation may be needed.

### Story 4 — Interaction or Next Step

Use one:

- Poll
- Question box
- Article link
- Save or follow invitation

Do not place several interaction stickers on one screen.

---

## 19. Reel Structure

A practical internal Reel structure may use:

### Opening

- State the question quickly
- Avoid exaggerated shock
- Avoid unsupported “three signs” framing

### Orientation

- Define the topic
- Correct the main misconception

### Explanation

- Explain one mechanism or clinical distinction
- Use plain language
- Preserve uncertainty

### Practical Meaning

- State what the viewer may reasonably do
- Include red flags when relevant

### Closing

- One CTA
- Link to deeper NervLock education
- No product pressure on disease content

A Reel should generally communicate one main idea.

---

## 20. Hook Standard

A hook should create relevance without fear.

### Preferred

- “브레인포그는 질병명일까요?”
- “잠을 자도 피곤한 이유는 하나가 아닙니다.”
- “손떨림이 모두 파킨슨병을 의미하지는 않습니다.”
- “이 연구 결과가 곧 치료 효과를 의미하는 것은 아닙니다.”

### Avoid

- “이 증상이 있으면 치매입니다.”
- “뇌가 망가지고 있다는 신호”
- “의사들이 알려주지 않는 사실”
- “이 영양제를 안 먹으면 늦습니다.”
- “당신의 뇌는 이미 늙고 있습니다.”

The hook must remain accurate when viewed without the full caption.

---

## 21. Caption Structure

A standard caption may contain:

1. Hook
2. Clear orientation
3. Main explanation
4. Evidence context
5. Uncertainty
6. Practical next step
7. Red flags when relevant
8. CTA
9. Reference cue
10. Commercial disclosure when applicable

Not every caption needs all elements.

The medical meaning must remain complete.

---

## 22. Caption Length

Caption length should follow the content.

Use shorter captions for:

- Simple definitions
- Story support
- Visual-first education

Use longer captions for:

- Evidence interpretation
- Disease education
- Research updates
- Product science

Do not create long captions through repetition.

Do not hide important safety information after unnecessary introductory text.

---

## 23. Korean Caption Tone

Korean captions should be:

- Professional
- Calm
- Respectful
- Clear
- Natural
- Not overly formal
- Not casual enough to weaken medical seriousness

Preferred patterns:

- “현재 근거를 종합하면…”
- “다만 이 결과를 모든 사람에게 적용할 수는 없습니다.”
- “이 증상만으로 특정 질환을 진단할 수는 없습니다.”
- “다음과 같은 경우에는 진료가 필요할 수 있습니다.”

Avoid:

- “무조건”
- “반드시 좋아집니다”
- “완치”
- “기적”
- “충격”
- “소름”
- “절대 먹지 마세요” without a genuine safety reason

---

## 24. English Caption Tone

English captions should be:

- Clear
- Evidence-calibrated
- Professional
- Accessible
- Natural for the intended audience

Preferred patterns:

- “Current evidence suggests…”
- “This does not mean that…”
- “The findings may not apply to…”
- “Persistent or progressive symptoms should be evaluated.”

Avoid translating Korean phrasing mechanically.

English content is a separate editorial asset.

## 25. CTA Hierarchy

Preferred CTA order:

1. Learn
2. Save
3. Share
4. Read more
5. Follow
6. Ask a general question
7. Review evidence
8. Review product information
9. Purchase

Purchase must not dominate education.

### Social CTA Standard

Use no more than:

- One Save CTA
- One Follow CTA

Preferred action area:

- “🔖 저장”
- “➕ 팔로우”

Do not repeat save prompts.

Do not combine multiple competing actions in one post.

## 31. Content Risk Classification

Use the NervLock four-level system.

### Level 0 — Administrative

Examples:

- Schedule announcement
- Website maintenance notice

### Level 1 — General Education

Examples:

- Exercise
- Sleep hygiene
- Basic anatomy

### Level 2 — Medical Education

Examples:

- Symptom guide
- Disease explanation
- Study interpretation
- Supplement evidence discussion

### Level 3 — High-Risk Clinical or Commercial

Examples:

- Product claims
- Dose recommendations
- Physician product endorsement
- Testimonials
- Before-and-after
- Paid advertisements
- Disease-related commercial content

### Level 4 — Prohibited

Examples:

- Disease-treatment supplement claim
- Guaranteed prevention
- Fake patient outcome
- Advice to stop medication
- False physician endorsement

Risk level determines workflow and approval.

## 32. Medical Education Standard

Every medical post should answer relevant parts of:

- What is the issue?
- What may cause it?
- What does current evidence suggest?
- What remains uncertain?
- What may be reasonable?
- When should care be considered?

Symptom posts must not encourage self-diagnosis.

Disease posts must not become product funnels.

---

## 33. Red-Flag Content

Red flags should be included when omission could create harm.

Examples may involve:

- Sudden neurological deficit
- New severe headache
- Altered consciousness
- Acute weakness
- New speech difficulty
- Rapidly progressive symptoms
- Severe medication reaction

The exact red flags must be topic-specific and medically reviewed.

Use proportionate language.

Do not use emergency content to increase engagement.

---

## 34. Evidence Communication

Social posts should distinguish:

- Established evidence
- Supported but qualified evidence
- Emerging evidence
- Hypothesis
- Insufficient evidence

Do not use:

- “Science proves”
- “Clinically proven”
- “Research says”

without precise support and regulatory review.

Mechanism must remain separate from clinical outcome.

Association must remain separate from causation.

---

## 35. Reference Display

Social posts should maintain traceability.

Options include:

- Short source line
- Reference slide
- Caption references
- Link to the canonical article
- Website evidence page

For a study-specific post, record:

- Exact study
- Publication year
- Study type
- Population
- Main limitation

Do not cite a search snippet.

Do not use AI-generated citations without verification.

---

## 36. Research News

A new study post should state:

- Study type
- Population
- Intervention or exposure
- Main result
- Important limitation
- Whether findings are preliminary
- Whether clinical practice changes

Avoid:

- Presenting one study as consensus
- Repeating press-release language
- Ignoring absolute effect
- Ignoring safety
- Ignoring contradictory evidence

Preprints must be labeled.

---

## 37. Disease Language

Disease names may be used in genuine education.

Risk increases when disease language appears with:

- Product
- Purchase link
- Discount
- Physician recommendation
- Testimonial
- Ingredient mechanism
- “Solution” wording

Avoid:

> Disease fear → mechanism → ingredient → product → purchase

A disclaimer does not neutralize a product-centered disease post.

---

## 38. Product Separation

Product content must remain visually and editorially separate from disease education.

Required controls:

- Clear commercial label
- Approved product category
- Approved claim language
- Safety information
- Product science link
- No disease-treatment implication
- No automatic product CTA at the end of disease posts
- Separate review workflow

The audience should understand whether a post is:

- Education
- Product science
- Product information
- Advertisement

---

## 39. Product Content Ratio

Before product launch:

- Product content should be absent or limited to transparent development philosophy and category education.

After launch:

- Product content should remain a minority of the total channel.
- The active ratio should be documented in the content calendar.
- Product posts must not displace high-value education.

A product launch campaign may temporarily increase product content, but the channel must remain recognizably educational.

## 41. Physician Identity

Physician identity may establish:

- Authorship
- Accountability
- Relevant expertise
- Educational context

It must not create:

- Guarantee
- Universal recommendation
- Product pressure
- Medicine-like authority for a food or supplement

Avoid:

- “신경과 전문의가 보장합니다”
- “의사가 만들었으니 효과가 확실합니다”
- White-coat product holding
- Physician image next to prohibited claims

---

## 42. Personal Brand Boundary

The neurologist may appear as:

- Educator
- Evidence interpreter
- Medical reviewer
- Brand founder

The account should not become dependent on:

- Private family content
- Unrelated lifestyle exposure
- Personal authority without evidence
- Daily self-promotion

Human presence may increase trust.

It should support, not replace, the NervLock platform identity.

---

## 43. Testimonials

Testimonials are not evidence.

Before use, confirm:

- Authenticity
- Consent
- Commercial disclosure
- Typicality
- Editing integrity
- Regulatory permission
- Privacy

Do not use:

- AI-generated patient stories presented as real
- Disease-treatment testimonials
- Medication replacement stories
- Exaggerated exceptional outcomes
- Fake review counts

---

## 44. Before-and-After Content

Before-and-after content is presumptively high risk.

For NervLock medical education and products, it should generally be avoided.

It may not be used without:

- Medical review
- Regulatory review
- Consent review
- Image-integrity review
- Final human approval

Do not use different lighting, angle, posture, or editing to exaggerate change.

---

## 45. Patient Cases

Patient cases require:

- Valid educational purpose
- De-identification
- Consent where required
- Privacy review
- Medical review
- Regulatory review
- No misleading outcome claim

Changing a name alone is not sufficient de-identification.

Avoid combining patient cases with a product CTA.

---

## 46. Comments

Comments are part of the public content environment.

NervLock should:

- Answer general educational questions
- Clarify misunderstandings
- Correct harmful misinformation
- Maintain respectful discussion
- Avoid individualized diagnosis
- Avoid individualized treatment
- Avoid medication changes
- Escalate safety concerns

## 47. Comment Response Categories

### General Education

Provide a concise answer and link to deeper education.

### Individual Symptom or Diagnosis Request

State that a diagnosis cannot be made through comments.

Provide general boundaries and appropriate care guidance.

### Medication Question

Do not recommend starting, stopping, or changing prescription medication.

Encourage discussion with the treating clinician.

### Urgent Red Flag

Use a clear urgent-care response.

Do not continue a prolonged public discussion.

### Product Complaint

Route to the approved support pathway.

### Adverse Event

Route immediately to the adverse-event process.

### Abuse or Harassment

Moderate according to policy.

## 101. Final Social Media Principles

Social media is not a diagnostic clinic.

A short caption is not exempt from evidence standards.

A physician title is not a product guarantee.

A disclaimer cannot repair a misleading post.

A Reel should not turn disease fear into product demand.

A carousel should teach one coherent lesson.

A Story is still public medical communication.

A pinned comment is still part of the claim.

A paid boost changes the regulatory context.

A testimonial is not evidence.

A trend is not a strategic reason.

A high-view post is not successful when it creates misunderstanding.

The NervLock social system should consistently move the audience through:

**Question**  
↓  
**Explanation**  
↓  
**Evidence**  
↓  
**Practical Meaning**  
↓  
**Trust**  
↓  
**Deeper Education**

Products may appear only when they have a legitimate, transparent, and approved role within that sequence.
