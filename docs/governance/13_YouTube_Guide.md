## 1. Purpose

This document defines how NervLock must plan, research, script, produce, review, approve, publish, distribute, moderate, measure, update, correct, and archive YouTube content.

It governs:

- Long-form YouTube videos
- YouTube Shorts
- YouTube Live
- Premieres
- Channel trailers
- Featured videos
- Playlists
- Community posts related to video content
- Video titles
- Thumbnails
- Descriptions
- Chapters
- Captions
- Transcripts
- End screens
- Cards
- Pinned comments
- Product-related video content
- Medical education
- Disease and symptom education
- Research interpretation
- Product science
- Interviews
- Collaborations
- Sponsorships
- Paid promotion
- YouTube Shopping
- Video advertising
- Comment moderation
- Crisis communication
- Corrections
- Analytics
- AI-assisted production
- Video adaptation for Instagram, Facebook, Threads, and the NervLock website

The objective is not to maximize upload volume.

The objective is to build a durable, searchable, medically responsible brain health video library that strengthens public understanding and long-term trust.

## 2. Governing YouTube Principle

NervLock follows one central video rule:

> Every video must improve understanding without using fear, physician authority, or visual drama to create false certainty.

YouTube content may be more conversational than a clinical article.

It must not become:

- Less accurate
- Less cautious
- Less transparent
- Less safe
- More promotional
- More absolute

than the underlying evidence.

A compelling video is not successful when it:

- Encourages self-diagnosis
- Delays appropriate care
- Exaggerates a study
- Implies disease treatment by a product
- Uses a physician title as proof
- Uses a thumbnail that contradicts the actual conclusion
- Hides commercial relationships
- Creates a misleading overall impression

## 3. Strategic Role of YouTube

YouTube serves seven strategic functions within NervLock.

### 3.1 Deep Education

Explain topics that require more context than a social card or short caption can provide.

### 3.2 Search Discovery

Answer durable brain health questions in a format that may be discovered through YouTube and web search.

### 3.3 Human Trust

Allow viewers to understand the physician’s reasoning, communication style, and commitment to evidence.

### 3.4 Evidence Interpretation

Explain:

- What a study found
- What it did not find
- How certain the result is
- Whether it changes practice
- Whether it applies to the viewer

### 3.5 Medical Navigation

Help viewers recognize:

- Common explanations
- Important red flags
- When professional evaluation may be appropriate
- What online content cannot determine

### 3.6 Content Ecosystem

Connect:

- Long-form video
- Shorts
- NervLock Lab articles
- Brain Health Academy
- Instagram
- Facebook
- Threads
- Newsletters

### 3.7 Responsible Product Education

Explain product rationale, evidence, dose, safety, limitations, and quality only after the product has passed scientific, regulatory, and operational review.

YouTube is not a diagnostic clinic.

YouTube is not the primary product storefront of NervLock.

## 4. Channel Positioning

The NervLock YouTube channel should communicate:

**A neurologist-led, evidence-based brain health education channel that explains complex medical and scientific information clearly and responsibly.**

The channel should not primarily resemble:

- A supplement sales channel
- A clinic advertisement
- A personal lifestyle vlog
- A fear-based disease channel
- A generic wellness channel
- A biohacking channel
- A reaction-content channel
- An anonymous AI-content channel
- A sensational medical-news channel
- A product-review affiliate channel

The expected viewer response is:

> “I understand this topic more clearly, including what remains uncertain.”

not:

> “I am afraid and need to buy or book something immediately.”

## 7. Current Platform Baseline

YouTube platform rules change.

At this document’s review cutoff:

- YouTube Shorts may be up to three minutes.
- New square or vertical videos within the applicable duration may be categorized as Shorts according to current YouTube rules.
- NervLock’s internal Shorts production standard is 1080 × 1920, 9:16.
- YouTube medical misinformation policies apply across videos, descriptions, comments, live streams, and external links used in YouTube content.
- Paid product placements, sponsorships, endorsements, and other commercial relationships require applicable disclosure.
- Automatic captions should be reviewed and corrected before a video is treated as publication-ready.
- Video chapters may be added to improve navigation and context.
- Platform specifications and policies must be rechecked before major template, advertising, music, or distribution decisions.

Platform eligibility is not the same as NervLock approval.

## 8. Content Success Definition

A successful NervLock video should achieve one or more of the following:

- Improve understanding
- Correct a misconception
- Clarify evidence strength
- Explain uncertainty
- Identify a practical next step
- Explain when medical evaluation may be needed
- Become a durable resource
- Lead viewers to deeper education
- Generate high-quality questions
- Strengthen trust
- Reduce unsafe product assumptions

Views, subscribers, watch time, and revenue are secondary.

A high-performing video that creates medical misunderstanding is a failure.

## 17. Topic Selection Criteria

A video may proceed when it has:

- Clear viewer value
- Strategic fit
- Sufficient evidence
- Appropriate medical boundaries
- A useful visual or spoken explanation
- Sustainable update capacity
- Distinct contribution
- Acceptable regulatory risk

A topic should be rejected or reframed when:

- It depends on fear
- It requires an unsupported product claim
- It encourages self-diagnosis
- It cannot be explained safely in the intended duration
- It duplicates existing content without new value
- It exceeds NervLock’s competence
- It relies mainly on speculative mechanisms
- It disguises commercial content as neutral education
- It cannot be maintained when evidence changes

## 20. Evidence Search

For medical videos, prioritize:

1. Current clinical guidelines
2. Systematic reviews
3. Pivotal randomized trials
4. Major observational studies
5. Safety sources
6. Primary mechanistic studies where relevant
7. Official regulator or public health sources

Record:

- Search date
- Evidence cutoff
- Included sources
- Contradictory findings
- Safety evidence
- Retraction checks
- Full-text status
- Reviewer

The spoken script may be concise.

The evidence process must still be complete.

## 24. Opening Hook

A hook should create relevance without fear.

### Preferred

- “손떨림이 있다고 모두 파킨슨병은 아닙니다.”
- “브레인포그는 공식 진단명일까요?”
- “수면과 기억력의 관계는 생각보다 단순하지 않습니다.”
- “이 연구 결과가 곧 치료 효과를 의미하지는 않습니다.”
- “기억력이 떨어졌다고 느낄 때 먼저 구분해야 할 것이 있습니다.”

### Avoid

- “이 증상이 있으면 치매입니다.”
- “뇌가 망가지고 있다는 신호”
- “의사들이 숨기는 진실”
- “이 영양제를 안 먹으면 늦습니다.”
- “단 7일 만에 기억력이 회복됩니다.”

The hook must remain accurate when separated from the full video.

## 25. First 30 Seconds

The opening should quickly answer:

- What the video covers
- Why the viewer should continue
- What misconception will be corrected
- What the video will not claim

Avoid:

- Long logo animations
- Repetitive greetings
- Excessive biography
- Product placement
- Unverified statistics
- Fear-based montage

The viewer should understand the educational value early.

---

## 26. Script Tone

Write like an experienced neurologist speaking to an intelligent viewer.

The tone should be:

- Calm
- Precise
- Conversational
- Respectful
- Evidence-based
- Clear
- Honest about uncertainty
- Practical

Avoid:

- Academic lecture style without explanation
- Overly casual slang
- Dramatic certainty
- Moral judgment
- Patronizing language
- Repeated self-reference
- Authority pressure

---

## 27. Spoken Language Standard

Spoken language should:

- Use short sentences
- Define technical terms
- Avoid unexplained abbreviations
- Use transitions
- Repeat only essential takeaways
- Clearly mark uncertainty
- Clearly mark red flags
- Avoid verbal clutter

The spoken script must not rely on subtitles to correct a misleading statement.

## 28. Korean Script Standard

Preferred patterns:

- “현재 근거를 종합하면…”
- “다만 이 결과만으로 인과관계를 단정할 수는 없습니다.”
- “이 증상만으로 특정 질환을 진단할 수는 없습니다.”
- “다음과 같은 경우에는 진료가 필요할 수 있습니다.”
- “기전상 가능성과 실제 임상 효과는 구분해야 합니다.”

Avoid:

- “무조건”
- “완치”
- “기적”
- “100%”
- “반드시 좋아집니다”
- “이것만 하면 됩니다”
- “충격적인 사실”

## 29. English Script Standard

English scripts should:

- Use natural international English
- Preserve evidence qualifiers
- Avoid inflated marketing language
- Avoid literal translation from Korean
- Define medical terms
- State clinical boundaries clearly

Preferred:

- “Current evidence suggests…”
- “This does not establish causation.”
- “Clinical benefit has not been established.”
- “Persistent or progressive symptoms should be evaluated.”

## 30. Evidence Language in Video

### High Certainty

Use:

- Demonstrates
- Reduces
- Increases
- Is recommended

Specify:

- Population
- Outcome
- Conditions

### Moderate Certainty

Use:

- Probably
- Likely
- Evidence suggests

### Low Certainty

Use:

- May
- Might
- Limited evidence suggests

### Very Low Certainty

Use:

- It is uncertain
- A hypothesis has been proposed
- Clinical benefit has not been established

The thumbnail and title may not use stronger language than the script.

## 31. Mechanism Explanation

When showing a biological mechanism:

1. State the evidence type
2. Identify whether it is established or proposed
3. Explain the pathway
4. State whether a clinical outcome was demonstrated
5. Avoid product efficacy inference

Preferred statement:

> “이 성분이 이 경로에 관여할 수 있다는 점과 실제로 환자에게 의미 있는 효과가 확인되었다는 것은 별개의 문제입니다.”

Mechanism animation must not become a visual guarantee.

---

## 32. Association and Causation

Observational findings should be described as:

- Associated with
- Linked to
- Observed together
- Correlated with

State when:

- Reverse causation is possible
- Confounding remains
- Causality is not established

Do not convert:

> “People with X had a higher rate of Y”

into:

> “X causes Y.”

---

## 33. Risk and Statistics

Explain when possible:

- Baseline risk
- Absolute risk
- Relative risk
- Time frame
- Population
- Confidence interval
- Clinical meaning

Avoid:

- Percentages without denominators
- “X times higher” without context
- Dramatic animated numbers
- Truncated graphs
- Unsupported population generalization

A numerical hook must be traceable to a verified source.

## 34. Symptom Videos

A symptom video should generally include:

- What the symptom means
- Common causes
- Important causes
- Questions that change interpretation
- Red flags
- When to seek care
- What viewers should not assume
- Limits of online education

Do not imply diagnosis from:

- One symptom
- A checklist
- A short self-test
- A viewer comment

## 35. Disease Videos

Disease videos should separate:

- Definition
- Symptoms
- Diagnosis
- Treatment
- Prognosis
- Lifestyle and supportive care
- Caregiver issues
- Uncertainty

Do not:

- Lead directly to a product
- Use disease fear as a conversion method
- Imply that nutrition replaces standard treatment
- Use a dramatic patient image without purpose
- Overpromise prevention

---

## 36. Treatment Content

Treatment education must:

- Reflect current guidelines
- Distinguish standard care from emerging options
- Discuss major harms
- State that individual treatment requires clinical evaluation
- Avoid changing prescribed treatment through general content

Do not tell viewers to:

- Stop medication
- Change dose
- Replace treatment
- Delay evaluation

without an appropriate direct clinical relationship and legal context.

---

## 37. Supplement and Ingredient Videos

A supplement video should explain:

- The intended question
- Ingredient identity
- Studied form
- Studied dose
- Population
- Outcome
- Evidence certainty
- Safety
- Interactions
- What remains uncertain
- Whether the finished product has direct evidence

Do not:

- Treat mechanistic evidence as efficacy
- Treat one positive trial as consensus
- Ignore negative trials
- Use disease-treatment framing
- Use “doctor approved” as proof
- Hide commercial interest

---

Product science videos require:

- Approved product classification

- Evidence dossier
- Safety review

- Regulatory review
- Final human approval

1. What the product is
2. Who it was designed for
3. Who should use caution
4. What it contains
5. Why each ingredient was selected
6. What the evidence supports
7. What the evidence does not establish
8. Safety
9. Quality

11. Commercial disclosure

## 39. Product Placement

A product may appear in educational content only when:

- The product is relevant
- The video remains useful without purchase
- The commercial relationship is clear
- The approved claim territory is preserved
- The product does not appear beside urgent medical warnings
- The visual does not imply treatment
- The description and links are reviewed

Avoid:

> Disease fear → product mechanism → product pack shot → purchase CTA

---

- Interview host

- Guaranteed outcome
- Universal recommendation
- Regulatory approval
- Product efficacy
- Replacement of medical care

- Holding a product in a white coat
- Pointing at disease-reversal graphics
- “전문의가 보장”

## 41. Patient and Caregiver Stories

Patient or caregiver stories require:

- Valid educational purpose
- Consent
- Privacy review
- De-identification
- Accurate representation
- No fabricated outcome
- No product pressure
- Regulatory review where applicable

## 45. YouTube Shorts Strategy

Shorts should function as:

- Discovery
- Concept introduction
- Myth correction
- Red-flag education
- Bridge to a full video or article

Shorts should not be used for:

- Complex individualized treatment
- Full product substantiation
- Dense dose comparisons
- Long safety discussions
- Nuanced legal claims
- High-risk case analysis

One Short should communicate one main idea.

## 46. Shorts Duration

YouTube currently supports Shorts up to three minutes.

NervLock internal targets:

### 15–30 Seconds

Use for:

- Definition
- One misconception
- One red flag
- One key distinction

### 30–60 Seconds

Use for:

- Focused explanation
- One study takeaway
- One practical action

### 60–180 Seconds

Use only when:

- The added time is necessary
- Retention remains acceptable
- Medical context cannot be compressed safely
- Music and rights are cleared
- The format is confirmed as a Short before publication

Do not lengthen a Short to use the full platform limit.

## 47. Shorts Structure

### Opening

State the question immediately.

### Core Explanation

Provide one accurate distinction.

### Qualification

State key uncertainty or boundary.

### Practical Meaning

State one reasonable next step.

### Closing

Use one CTA.

Example:

> “손떨림이 있다고 모두 파킨슨병은 아닙니다. 약물, 긴장, 본태떨림 등 여러 원인이 있을 수 있고, 떨림의 양상과 동반 증상이 중요합니다. 새롭게 악화되거나 움직임이 느려지는 증상이 동반된다면 진료를 고려하세요.”

## 53. B-Roll

- Support the explanation

- Reduce visual monotony
- Remain medically accurate

- False hospital settings
- Product placement unrelated to the sentence
- Images that imply causality

## 54. Medical Graphics

Medical graphics must identify whether they are:

- Anatomical
- Diagnostic
- Mechanistic
- Conceptual
- Illustrative
- AI-generated

Do not show:

- Plaque disappearing after a product
- Neurons regenerating after a supplement
- Brain aging reversing
- Fake patient scans
- Product crossing the blood–brain barrier as proven fact

A mechanism animation does not prove clinical benefit.

- Remain on screen long enough

- Evidence level
- Red flags
- Takeaways

## 69. Medical Disclaimer

A disclaimer should clarify that the content is:

- General education
- Not individualized diagnosis
- Not individualized treatment
- Not a substitute for professional care

The disclaimer must not be used to justify:

- Unsafe advice
- Prohibited product claims
- Misleading title
- Inadequate red flags
- Medication changes

Urgent guidance should be stated directly in the video.

## 70. Commercial Disclosure

Disclose when:

- NervLock sells the product
- The speaker has a financial interest
- A sponsor supported the video
- A product was provided
- An affiliate relationship exists
- YouTube Shopping is used
- The content contains paid promotion

Disclosure should be:

- Clear
- Proximate
- Verbal when appropriate
- Visible in description
- Compatible with Korean law and YouTube requirements

Platform disclosure tools do not replace legal disclosure.

## 71. Paid Promotion

Any paid product placement, sponsorship, endorsement, free product arrangement, or material commercial relationship requires review.

Before publication:

- Activate the applicable YouTube paid-promotion disclosure
- Add legally appropriate disclosure
- Review script
- Review title
- Review thumbnail
- Review description
- Review product link
- Review targeting and promotion
- Archive the approved version

A sponsored video must not have weaker medical standards than an unsponsored video.

## 72. YouTube Shopping

- Product eligibility is confirmed
- Product classification is confirmed
- Claims are approved
- The landing page is compliant
- Commercial disclosure is clear
- Safety information is accessible
- The video’s educational value remains intact

Do not tag a product in:

- Emergency content
- Red-flag content
- Disease diagnosis videos
- Treatment sections
- Patient stories
- Unsupported supplement discussions

Product tagging changes the commercial context.

## 73. Advertising and Promote

A video approved for organic education is not automatically approved for paid promotion.

Before using YouTube Promote or Google Ads:

- Reclassify as advertising
- Review all claims
- Review thumbnail
- Review description
- Review landing page
- Review audience targeting
- Review physician identity
- Confirm required Korean self-review or advertising review
- Archive the ad version

Paid distribution can change the regulatory interpretation.

## 74. Medical Misinformation

NervLock must not publish content that:

- Promotes harmful remedies
- Contradicts established high-quality medical evidence without adequate context
- Encourages viewers to reject necessary care
- Presents unsupported treatment as established
- Uses manipulated media to create false medical claims
- Links viewers to prohibited or harmful treatment content

Educational, scientific, or documentary context does not excuse a misleading overall impression.

External links are part of the content environment.

7. Add a visible correction
8. Update the canonical article
9. Update derivative Shorts and social assets
10. Notify reviewers
11. Record the correction

## 118. Final YouTube Principles

YouTube is not a diagnostic clinic.

A thumbnail is part of the medical claim.

A title may not be stronger than the video.

A Short may not become more certain than the long-form source.

A physician title is not evidence.

A mechanism animation is not proof.

A patient story is not a clinical trial.

A disclaimer does not repair a misleading video.

A paid relationship must be disclosed.

A platform disclosure does not replace legal disclosure.

A product should not appear at the end of every disease video.

Automatic captions are drafts.

High watch time does not prove educational quality.

A viral video is not a strategic success when it creates medical misunderstanding.

NervLock YouTube should move viewers through:

**Question**  
↓  
**Clear Explanation**  
↓  
**Evidence**  
↓  
**Uncertainty**  
↓  
**Practical Meaning**  
↓  
**Medical Boundary**  
↓  
**Deeper Education**  
↓  
**Trust**

Products may appear only when they have a legitimate, transparent, and approved role within this sequence.
