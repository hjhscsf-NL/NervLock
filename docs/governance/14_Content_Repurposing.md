## 1. Purpose

This document defines how NervLock must transform one approved content asset into multiple derivative assets without weakening:

- Scientific accuracy
- Medical safety
- Regulatory compliance
- Reference integrity
- Brand consistency
- Reader comprehension
- Search value
- Accessibility
- Commercial transparency

It governs:

- Blog-to-Instagram adaptation
- Blog-to-Reels adaptation
- Blog-to-YouTube adaptation
- YouTube-to-Shorts adaptation
- YouTube-to-blog adaptation
- Article-to-newsletter adaptation
- Article-to-Threads adaptation
- Long-form-to-carousel adaptation
- Carousel-to-Story adaptation
- Korean-to-English localization
- English-to-Korean localization
- Website-to-Naver Blog adaptation
- Research review-to-public education adaptation
- Product science-to-product page adaptation
- Product science-to-social media adaptation
- Transcript-to-article adaptation
- Live session-to-edited content adaptation
- Interview-to-quote-card adaptation
- Internal evidence review-to-public content adaptation
- AI-assisted summarization and rewriting
- Cross-platform reuse
- Content refresh
- Correction propagation
- Archive and withdrawal of derivative assets

The purpose of repurposing is to extend the educational value of approved content.

It is not to produce more content from less review.

---

## 2. Governing Repurposing Principle

NervLock follows one central repurposing rule:

> A derivative asset may become shorter, simpler, or more visual, but it must never become stronger, broader, more certain, more commercial, or less safe than its approved source.

Repurposing may change:

- Format
- Length
- Tone within approved boundaries
- Visual structure
- Level of detail
- CTA
- Channel-native organization

Repurposing must not change without new review:

- Evidence conclusion
- Certainty
- Population
- Outcome
- Dose
- Safety
- Red flags
- Product claim
- Commercial status
- Medical boundary
- Conflict disclosure
- Reference meaning

---

## 3. Strategic Role of Repurposing

Repurposing should help NervLock:

- Reinforce the same core educational message across channels
- Reach different audiences in appropriate formats
- Connect short-form discovery to long-form education
- Reduce duplicated research work
- Preserve consistent claims
- Build durable content clusters
- Improve website, social, and video coherence
- Extend the useful life of high-quality content
- Maintain topic continuity
- Reduce unreviewed improvisation

Repurposing is a controlled extension of one approved source.

It is not a license to create an unlimited content factory.

---

## 4. Source-of-Truth Principle

Every derivative asset must have one identified source of truth.

Preferred source hierarchy:

1. Approved NervLock evidence review
2. Approved NervLock Lab article
3. Approved product evidence dossier
4. Approved YouTube long-form script
5. Approved guideline or research briefing
6. Approved internal content brief
7. Approved interview or Live transcript

A derivative asset must not be based solely on:

- Another derivative asset
- An unapproved caption
- A screenshot
- An AI summary
- A viral post
- A competitor’s content
- A search snippet
- An old draft

When possible, all derivatives should point back to the highest-level approved source.

---

## 5. Canonical Content

The canonical content asset is the authoritative editorial source for a topic.

It should normally contain:

- Full context
- Evidence level
- Medical review
- References
- Limitations
- Red flags
- Update date
- Conflict disclosure
- Product boundary

For durable medical content, the canonical asset is usually:

- NervLock Lab article
- Brain Health Academy article
- Product science page
- Evidence review page

For video-first topics, the canonical asset may be:

- Approved long-form video script
- Published video with preserved transcript
- Companion website page

The canonical asset must be recorded in every derivative brief.

## 6. Repurposing Versus Rewriting

### Repurposing

Preserves the same core medical and scientific conclusion while changing format.

Examples:

- Article → carousel
- Video → Short
- Evidence review → infographic

### Rewriting

Changes structure, emphasis, audience, or conclusion enough to require a new editorial review.

Examples:

- General article → product sales page
- Professional evidence review → consumer dose recommendation
- Disease education → commercial landing page
- Korean article → English article for a different jurisdiction
- Interview answer → definitive medical guidance

When uncertain, classify the task as rewriting rather than simple repurposing.

---

## 7. Repurposing Risk Classification

### Level 0 — Administrative Conversion

Examples:

- Approved article → internal slide summary
- Approved video → transcript cleanup
- Approved content → archive index

Required review:

- Basic accuracy
- Version control

### Level 1 — Low-Risk Format Adaptation

Examples:

- General lifestyle article → educational carousel
- Approved definition → static card
- Approved website summary → newsletter intro

Required review:

- Editorial
- Brand
- Basic factual check

### Level 2 — Medical Adaptation

Examples:

- Symptom article → Reel
- Disease article → carousel
- Evidence review → Short
- Guideline article → infographic

Required review:

- Scientific
- Medical safety
- Reference
- Brand
- Channel review

### Level 3 — Commercial or High-Risk Adaptation

Examples:

- Product dossier → product page
- Disease education → sponsored social post
- Physician interview → product advertisement
- Product science → influencer script
- Long-form content → paid campaign
- Testimonial extraction
- Before-and-after reuse

Required review:

- Scientific
- Medical
- Regulatory
- Reference
- Brand
- Final human approval

### Level 4 — Prohibited Adaptation

Examples:

- Disease article → disease-treatment supplement ad
- Mechanism section → guaranteed outcome claim
- Patient story → unapproved testimonial
- Educational Short → medication-change instruction
- Old study summary → current clinical recommendation without update

Do not create.

## 8. Repurposing Decision Gate

Before repurposing, confirm:

1. Is the source approved?
2. Is the source current?
3. Is the source suitable for the target channel?
4. Is the intended audience defined?
5. Is the target format capable of preserving the necessary nuance?
6. Is the medical and regulatory risk acceptable?
7. Is the derivative useful rather than repetitive?
8. Is the canonical source recorded?
9. Is an update path available?
10. Is final review capacity available?

If the source is outdated, update the source first.

## 10. Fidelity Principle

Every derivative asset must preserve fidelity in five dimensions.

### 10.1 Claim Fidelity

The claim must mean the same thing.

### 10.2 Certainty Fidelity

The language must not become more confident.

### 10.3 Population Fidelity

The target population must not become broader.

### 10.4 Outcome Fidelity

A biomarker, mechanism, or surrogate must not become a patient-important outcome.

### 10.5 Safety Fidelity

Warnings, exclusions, and care boundaries must remain proportionate.

Failure in any dimension requires revision or a new evidence review.

---

## 11. Compression Rule

Shortening creates risk because qualifiers are often removed first.

When compressing:

- Preserve the core conclusion
- Preserve the key limitation
- Preserve essential uncertainty
- Preserve red flags
- Preserve commercial disclosure
- Preserve product limitations
- Remove lower-priority background first

Do not remove:

- “may”
- “in some populations”
- “observational”
- “does not prove causation”
- “not a diagnosis”
- “seek care when…”
- “finished-product evidence is limited”

when those phrases are material to accuracy.

---

## 12. Expansion Rule

Expanding a source requires caution.

Do not add:

- New claims
- New statistics
- New mechanisms
- New recommendations
- New product comparisons
- New dose guidance
- New safety statements

without evidence and review.

Expansion that introduces new factual content is not simple repurposing.

It is a new content-development task.

---

## 13. Headline and Hook Fidelity

A derivative title or hook may be:

- Shorter
- More conversational
- More question-based
- More channel-native

It may not be:

- More certain
- More alarming
- More diagnostic
- More commercial
- More absolute

### Source

> “Some observational studies report an association between poor sleep and cognitive decline, but causality is not established.”

### Acceptable Short Hook

> “잠이 부족하면 기억력도 떨어질까요?”

### Unacceptable Hook

> “잠 부족이 치매를 부릅니다.”

The hook must be accurate when seen without the full content.

---

## 14. CTA Fidelity

The CTA may change according to channel.

### Educational Source

Permitted derivative CTAs:

- Read more
- Save
- Share
- Watch the full explanation
- Review the evidence
- Discuss persistent symptoms with a clinician

### Product Science Source

Permitted derivative CTAs:

- Review ingredients
- Review safety
- Read product science
- Check who should use caution

### Commercial Source

A purchase CTA requires:

- Approved commercial context
- Approved claims
- Clear disclosure
- Appropriate safety access
- Regulatory review

A disease education source must not acquire a purchase CTA merely because the target channel supports shopping.

- Current and Approved
- Current With Minor Update Needed
- Major Update Required
- Under Review
- Superseded
- Archived
- Withdrawn

Only `Current and Approved` sources may be repurposed directly.

## 18. One-to-Many Content Model

One approved pillar article may generate:

- One long-form YouTube video
- Two to four Shorts
- One Instagram carousel
- Two to four Stories
- One Threads sequence
- One Facebook adaptation
- One newsletter summary
- One infographic
- One FAQ expansion
- One quote card

This is a planning model, not a mandatory quota.

Create only derivative assets that add channel-specific value.

## 19. Content Atomization

A long-form source may be divided into content atoms.

Examples:

- Definition
- Core distinction
- Evidence takeaway
- Common misconception
- Practical action
- Red flag
- Study limitation
- Caregiver tip
- Product limitation
- FAQ

Every atom should preserve:

- Context
- Certainty
- Population
- Safety
- Traceability

An atom that becomes misleading without surrounding text should not be isolated.

---

## 20. Safe Content Atoms

Generally suitable:

- Clear definitions
- Stable distinctions
- One practical action
- One red flag
- One evidence limitation
- One myth correction
- One study-design explanation
- One safety reminder

Higher-risk atoms:

- Numerical risk
- Dose
- Treatment comparison
- Product efficacy
- Subgroup finding
- Mechanism
- Patient outcome
- Testimonial
- Diagnostic criterion

Higher-risk atoms require stronger context and review.

## 21. Blog to Instagram Carousel

Recommended workflow:

1. Identify the article’s primary question
2. Extract the bottom-line answer
3. Select one coherent teaching sequence
4. Retain the key limitation
5. Add red flags when relevant
6. Convert paragraphs into one-message slides
7. Build a cover that does not overstate
8. Add a final educational CTA
9. Link to the canonical article
10. Review the visual and caption together

### Suggested Structure

1. Cover
2. Definition
3. Why it matters
4. Main explanation
5. Evidence
6. Limitation
7. Practical meaning
8. Red flags
9. CTA

Do not convert an entire article into tiny text across ten cards.

---

## 22. Blog to Instagram Story

Use Stories for:

- One question
- One distinction
- One red flag
- One practical action
- One article bridge

### Four-Story Model

1. Question
2. Explanation
3. Practical meaning or red flag
4. Link or interaction

Do not compress a complex disease article into one unqualified Story.

---

## 23. Blog to Reel

Recommended workflow:

1. Select one core question
2. Write a direct opening
3. State the answer early
4. Explain one mechanism or distinction
5. Add one limitation
6. Add one practical next step
7. Link to the canonical source

A Reel should not attempt to reproduce the entire article.

The Reel must not become more certain than the article.

---

## 24. Blog to Threads

Threads may adapt an article into:

- One concise post
- A short sequence
- Myth and correction
- Evidence and limitation
- Question and answer

The opening post must remain accurate alone.

Do not place the qualification only in the last reply when the first post is misleading.

---

## 25. Blog to Facebook

Facebook adaptation may include:

- Longer explanatory copy
- Caregiver context
- Discussion question
- Article link
- Selected infographic

Avoid copying Instagram hashtags and line breaks automatically.

Review:

- Audience
- Link context
- Caption length
- Product proximity
- Comment risk

---

## 26. Blog to Newsletter

A newsletter adaptation should include:

- Why the topic matters
- Bottom-line conclusion
- One important uncertainty
- One practical next step
- Link to full article

Do not turn a neutral article into a sales email without reclassification and review.

---

## 27. Blog to YouTube Long-Form

Recommended workflow:

1. Convert the article’s argument into a spoken narrative
2. Define the viewer question
3. Place the bottom line early
4. Use examples and transitions
5. Convert tables into understandable visuals
6. Preserve uncertainty
7. Include red flags
8. Preserve references in description and transcript
9. Link to the article
10. Review the rough cut for changed meaning

A spoken script is not a paragraph-by-paragraph reading of the article.

---

## 28. Blog to YouTube Short

Select only one atom:

- Definition
- Misconception
- Red flag
- Evidence takeaway
- Practical distinction

Do not use a Short for:

- Full dose evaluation
- Complex differential diagnosis
- Product claim substantiation
- High-risk treatment guidance

---

## 29. YouTube to Blog

A transcript must not be published as an article without editing.

Required steps:

1. Verify transcript
2. Remove spoken repetition
3. Restructure headings
4. Add citations near claims
5. Add author and reviewer information
6. Add evidence cutoff
7. Add red-flag structure
8. Add internal links
9. Add metadata
10. Conduct full editorial review

The video may contain conversational shorthand that is unsuitable for permanent written publication.

---

## 30. YouTube to Shorts

Select moments that:

- Stand alone accurately
- Preserve context
- Do not rely on missing earlier explanation
- Do not remove a qualification
- Do not misrepresent the speaker

Do not create a provocative clip by removing the sentence that follows.

Each Short should link back to:

- Full video
- Relevant playlist
- Canonical article

---

## 31. YouTube to Reels

Review:

- Aspect ratio
- Subtitle safe zone
- Cover
- Opening speed
- CTA
- Caption
- Platform music rights
- Product disclosure
- Medical context

A YouTube clip is not automatically ready for Instagram.

## 32. YouTube to Quote Card

A quote may be used only when:

- It is exact
- It is understandable alone
- It remains accurate
- Speaker identity is correct
- It is not edited to intensify certainty
- It is not a product endorsement without review

Paraphrases must not be placed inside quotation marks.

---

## 33. Live Session to Edited Content

A Live recording may contain:

- Unscripted wording
- Incomplete answers
- Personal questions
- Patient information
- Product statements
- Errors
- Speculation

Before reuse:

1. Review the full transcript
2. Remove personal information
3. Correct errors
4. Add context
5. Add disclosure
6. Re-review medical claims
7. Re-review product claims
8. Create a correction note when needed
9. Obtain participant rights
10. Approve the final edit

A Live recording is not automatically an approved source.

- Full video
- Edited interview
- Article

- Newsletter

- Confirm guest approval when contractually required
- Preserve conflict disclosure
- Avoid transforming opinion into consensus

- Define the audience
- Explain limitations

A Product Evidence Dossier may generate:

- Product science page
- Product page
- FAQ
- Social product education
- YouTube product science video
- Customer support script
- Influencer brief

It must preserve:

- Exact ingredient identity
- Exact dose
- Safety
- Target population
- Exclusions
- Evidence limitations
- Finished-product evidence status
- Approved claim territory

## 38. Product Page to Social Media

A product page may generate social content only when:

- The product is launched or approved for launch
- Claims are approved
- Label is final
- Safety is accessible
- Commercial disclosure is clear
- Product science source is linked
- Content remains subordinate to education

Do not create disease-targeted product social posts from disease education pages.

## 39. Product Science to Influencer Brief

An influencer brief must contain:

- Approved product identity
- Approved claims
- Prohibited claims
- Required disclosure
- Safety
- Who should use caution
- Exact CTA
- Required links
- Correction obligations
- Content approval process

Influencers must not improvise stronger claims.

## 40. Website to Naver Blog

When adapting a website article to Naver Blog:

- Preserve medical meaning
- Use a more platform-native opening
- Avoid exact full-text duplication where possible
- Preserve red flags
- Preserve uncertainty
- Preserve references or source link
- Preserve author identity
- Link to the canonical article when useful
- Use a distinct title
- Record the update relationship

Naver adaptation must not become more sensational.

---

## 41. Naver Blog to Website

A Naver Blog post may become a website article only after:

- Evidence review
- Reference verification
- Structural rewrite
- SEO review
- Author and reviewer information
- Canonical decision
- Medical and regulatory review
- Update-date assignment

Naver copy is not automatically suitable as canonical long-form knowledge.

## 46. Claim Transformation Matrix

### Established Evidence

May remain:

- Demonstrates
- Reduces
- Is recommended

only within the same population and outcome.

### Supported but Qualified

May remain:

- Evidence suggests
- Probably
- Likely

Do not transform into:

- Proven
- Guaranteed
- Prevents

### Emerging Evidence

May remain:

- Early research suggests
- May
- Has been proposed

Do not transform into:

- Improves
- Protects
- Works

### Mechanistic Evidence

May remain:

- Participates in
- May influence a pathway
- Has a biological rationale

Do not transform into:

- Produces clinical benefit
- Prevents disease
- Repairs the brain

## 48. Aspect-Ratio Adaptation

For each target format:

- Recompose
- Reposition
- Recheck safe zones
- Recheck typography
- Recheck image crop
- Recheck CTA
- Recheck platform controls

Important content must remain readable.

---

## 49. Caption Adaptation

A caption should be rewritten for the channel.

Preserve:

- Main conclusion
- Qualification
- Red flags
- Disclosure
- Canonical link

Adjust:

- Length
- Opening
- CTA
- Formatting
- Hashtags
- Discussion prompt

Do not copy hashtags across channels automatically.

---

## 50. Thumbnail and Cover Adaptation

The derivative cover must match the source conclusion.

Do not create:

- Stronger claim
- Fear-based image
- Product-centered cover for educational content
- Fake scan
- Disease reversal
- Unapproved physician endorsement

A thumbnail or cover is part of the claim.

---

## 51. Reference Adaptation

Different channels may display references differently.

### Website

- In-text citations
- Full reference list

### Social

- Short source cue
- Reference slide
- Canonical article link
- Full caption references when appropriate

### YouTube

- Description references
- Transcript
- Companion article

### Newsletter

- Key source
- Link to full article

The derivative must remain traceable.

---

## 52. Reference Integrity

Do not:

- Drop the source of a numerical claim
- Cite a review as if it were the primary trial
- Use a press release in place of the study
- Invent a shortened citation
- Change publication year
- Remove retraction status
- Add an unrelated citation for credibility

Reference display may change.

Reference meaning may not.

---

## 53. Commercial Reclassification

A derivative may become commercial when it adds:

- Product image
- Product name
- Purchase link
- Discount
- Coupon
- Sponsorship
- Affiliate link
- Physician recommendation
- Retargeting
- Paid distribution
- Shopping tag

Commercial reclassification triggers:

- Regulatory review
- Claim review
- Disclosure
- Landing-page review
- Archive of approved ad version

An educational source does not automatically authorize a commercial derivative.

---

## 54. Paid Distribution

Before boosting or advertising a derivative:

1. Reclassify as advertising
2. Review title
3. Review visual
4. Review copy
5. Review CTA
6. Review targeting
7. Review landing page
8. Review disclosure
9. Confirm required self-review
10. Archive the approved version

Organic approval is not paid-media approval.

---

## 55. Product Link Rule

A product link may be added only when:

- The derivative is appropriate for commercial context
- The product is relevant
- Claims are approved
- Safety information is accessible
- The commercial relationship is clear
- The article or video remains useful without purchase

Do not add a product link to:

- Emergency guidance
- Red-flag content
- Disease diagnosis content
- Patient case
- Medication guidance
- Fear-based content

---

## 56. Testimonial Repurposing

A testimonial may not be extracted casually from:

- Comment
- DM
- Review
- Interview
- Live session
- Email
- Patient story

Before use, confirm:

- Authenticity
- Permission
- Privacy
- Typicality
- Editing integrity
- Commercial disclosure
- Regulatory permission
- Medical claim

A short excerpt may become more misleading than the full statement.

---

## 57. Before-and-After Repurposing

Do not repurpose before-and-after material without full review.

Confirm:

- Same person
- Same angle
- Same lighting
- Same conditions
- No retouching
- No AI alteration
- Consent
- Typicality
- Causality
- Regulatory permission

For NervLock medical and product content, avoidance is generally preferred.

---

## 58. AI-Assisted Repurposing

AI may assist with:

- Summarization
- Format conversion
- Caption drafting
- Script adaptation
- Translation
- Metadata
- Content atomization
- Clip identification
- Checklist completion

- Decide which qualifications are unnecessary
- Strengthen claims
- Invent references
- Create fake quotes
- Create fake testimonials
- Create unreviewed product claims
- Publish automatically
- Approve its own derivative

AI output remains a draft.

Avoid prompts such as:

- “Make this viral”
- “Make it stronger”
- “Make it more persuasive”
- “Remove unnecessary caveats”
- “Turn this into a product ad”

without explicit safeguards.

## 69. Correction Propagation

When the source is corrected:

1. Identify all derivatives
2. Assess materiality
3. Correct or withdraw each derivative
4. Stop paid promotion
5. Update captions, thumbnails, descriptions, and links
6. Add correction notes where needed
7. Notify partners
8. Record completion

A source correction is incomplete until material derivatives are addressed.

---

## 70. Retraction Propagation

When a cited study is retracted or materially corrected:

- Flag the source
- Reassess the conclusion
- Review all derivative assets
- Remove unsupported claims
- Add visible correction when material
- Update references
- Re-review product claims
- Re-review paid content
- Record the decision

Do not leave a derivative live because the citation is not visible on the platform.

---

## 71. Safety Update Propagation

When safety information changes:

- Update canonical content first
- Update product pages
- Update videos
- Update descriptions
- Update social posts
- Update FAQs
- Update customer support
- Update influencer scripts
- Update ads
- Stop incompatible campaigns

Safety updates take priority over content schedule.

---

## 72. Product Change Propagation

When product formula, label, dose, packaging, or claim changes:

- Freeze repurposing from old assets
- Identify all product derivatives
- Update product science
- Update product page
- Update videos
- Update social assets
- Update support scripts
- Update influencer content
- Update store listings
- Archive old versions

Do not reuse an old pack shot after a material formula or label change.

---

Withdraw a derivative when:

- Source is withdrawn
- Claim is no longer supported
- Safety is outdated
- Product changed
- Rights expired
- Regulation changed
- Format creates unavoidable misunderstanding
- Correction is insufficient
- Privacy risk exists

- Date
- Reason
- Asset
- URL
- Replacement
- Approver

## 97. Prohibited Repurposing Patterns

Do not:

- Turn uncertainty into certainty
- Turn association into causation
- Turn mechanism into benefit
- Turn ingredient evidence into product proof
- Turn an educational article into an advertisement without review
- Turn a patient story into a product testimonial
- Turn a quote into endorsement
- Turn a disease page into a product funnel
- Turn a preliminary study into a clinical recommendation
- Turn a guideline into universal advice
- Turn a long explanation into a misleading hook
- Remove red flags
- Remove commercial disclosure
- Remove the source
- Publish AI output without review
- Reuse outdated assets after product or evidence change
- Translate regulatory language mechanically
- Use a platform trend to override brand standards

## 98. Minimum Repurposing Standard

A NervLock derivative is publication-ready only when it is:

- Traceable to an approved source
- Medically faithful
- Evidence-calibrated
- Regulatorily classified
- Properly disclosed
- Channel-appropriate
- Accessible
- Brand-consistent
- Rights-cleared
- Version-controlled
- Updateable
- Human-approved

Shorter does not mean safer.

More visual does not mean less regulated.

More engaging does not mean more certain.

More channels do not mean more authority.

## 99. Final Repurposing Principles

One approved source may create many assets.

It may not create many interpretations.

A derivative may simplify language.

It may not simplify away truth.

A Short may be brief.

It may not be contextless.

A carousel may be visual.

It may not convert mechanism into outcome.

A translation may be natural.

It may not change medical meaning.

A product link may be convenient.

It may not transform education into disease-based sales.

A correction must travel through the entire derivative system.

A source is not fully managed until its derivatives are mapped.

The objective of repurposing is not to publish everywhere.

The objective is to make the same responsible NervLock knowledge useful in the right form, for the right audience, on the right channel.
