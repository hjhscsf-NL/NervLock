## 1. Purpose

This document is the operational planning system for all NervLock publishing activity.

It records:

- What content will be created
- Why it is being created
- Who it is for
- Which channel will publish it
- Which canonical source governs it
- What evidence and review level it requires
- Which derivative assets will be produced
- Who owns each step
- When each step is due
- What is approved, blocked, delayed, published, updated, or archived

This calendar is not a list of ideas alone.

It is a controlled production and review schedule.

No item should enter a public publishing slot before:

- Strategic fit is confirmed
- Risk is classified
- Evidence needs are defined
- Required reviewers are assigned
- The content owner is identified
- Final approval capacity is available

---

## 2. Governing Calendar Principle

NervLock follows one central content-planning rule:

> The calendar must reflect the mission of NervLock, not the pressure of algorithms, competitors, trends, or product launch urgency.

The calendar should preserve the sequence:

**Brain Health**  
→ **Education**  
→ **Science**  
→ **Trust**  
→ **Products**

The calendar must not gradually transform NervLock into:

- A product catalog
- A symptom-fear account
- A trend-driven content farm
- A repetitive posting machine
- An unreviewed medical advice channel
- A physician-influencer sales account

---

## 3. Role of the Calendar

The calendar serves six functions.

### 3.1 Strategic Balance

Ensure that foundational education, symptoms, conditions, science, trust, and products remain balanced.

### 3.2 Production Control

Prevent content from entering production without a clear owner, source, and review path.

### 3.3 Channel Coordination

Coordinate website, blog, Instagram, Facebook, Threads, YouTube, Shorts, and newsletter assets.

### 3.4 Risk Control

Identify high-risk content before deadlines create pressure to bypass review.

### 3.5 Repurposing Control

Track how approved canonical content becomes derivative assets.

### 3.6 Update Control

Ensure that corrections, new evidence, regulatory changes, and product changes propagate through the content system.

## 9. Recommended Strategic Mix

The following ranges are planning guides.

### Before Product Launch

- 55–70% foundational brain health and lifestyle education
- 15–25% symptoms, conditions, pain, and caregiver education
- 10–20% science, evidence, and trust
- 0–10% product-development philosophy or category education
- 0% direct product sales content before approval

### After Product Launch

- 50–65% foundational education
- 15–25% symptoms, conditions, pain, and caregiver education
- 10–20% science, evidence, and trust
- 5–15% product science and commercial content

Product content must never dominate the full calendar.

These ranges should be reviewed monthly.

## 10. Content Priority Order

Prioritize content in this order:

1. Urgent safety or misinformation correction
2. Foundational brain health education
3. High-value symptom and disease education
4. Caregiver needs
5. Evidence-based lifestyle education
6. Strategic topic clusters
7. Guideline and evidence updates
8. Brand trust and editorial standards
9. Product-adjacent education
10. Product science
11. Trend-driven content

Commercial opportunity must not move a low-value topic above a high-priority educational or safety need.

## 13. Risk Classification

### Level 0 — Administrative

Examples:

- Schedule notice
- Internal process

### Level 1 — Low-Risk Education

Examples:

- Basic anatomy
- General exercise
- General sleep hygiene

### Level 2 — Medical Education

Examples:

- Symptom article
- Disease guide
- Guideline summary
- Supplement evidence
- Research interpretation

### Level 3 — High-Risk Clinical or Commercial

Examples:

- Product page
- Paid advertisement
- Dose recommendation
- Physician product endorsement
- Patient case
- Testimonial
- Before-and-after
- Product-linked disease content

### Level 4 — Prohibited

Examples:

- Guaranteed disease prevention
- Product treatment claim
- Dangerous medical advice
- Fabricated evidence
- Fake patient outcome

Risk level determines lead time and reviewers.

---

## 14. Lead-Time Standard

### Level 1

Recommended minimum:

- 5–10 working days

### Level 2

Recommended minimum:

- 10–20 working days

### Level 3

Recommended minimum:

- 20–40 working days

### Urgent Safety Content

May use an expedited workflow, but must still document:

- Source
- Medical review
- Regulatory review when applicable
- Final approval
- Correction plan

Do not schedule a high-risk asset before review capacity exists.

## 22. Canonical-First Scheduling

For medically substantive content, preferred sequence:

1. Evidence review
2. Canonical article or approved long-form source
3. Derivative brief
4. Social and video production
5. Final review
6. Coordinated publication
7. Update mapping

Short-form content may precede a canonical article only when:

- The topic is low risk
- The source is already approved
- The message is complete and safe
- A deeper source is not necessary

---

## 23. Topic Cluster Scheduling

Each quarter should prioritize a limited number of clusters.

Recommended approach:

- 1–2 major pillar areas
- 2–4 supporting cluster articles per pillar
- 1 long-form video where useful
- 2–6 social derivatives
- 1 evidence or trust asset
- 1 caregiver or practical asset when relevant

Avoid opening too many clusters simultaneously.

Topical depth is more valuable than disconnected volume.

## 42. Starter 12-Week Framework

The following framework is provisional.

It should be converted into a dated calendar only after workload, reviewer capacity, and existing website content are assessed.

### Weeks 1–2 — Trust and Foundation

Primary goals:

- Explain NervLock
- Establish the evidence-based platform identity
- Publish or finalize a Start Here pathway
- Prepare one foundational brain-health article

Possible assets:

- What is NervLock?
- What does brain health mean?
- How NervLock reviews science
- Instagram pinned trust posts
- Website Start Here page

### Weeks 3–4 — Sleep and Brain Health

Primary goals:

- Build one coherent sleep cluster
- Connect article, carousel, Stories, Reel, and optional YouTube

Possible assets:

- Sleep and memory
- When sleep problems need evaluation
- One sleep myth correction
- One practical sleep Story sequence

### Weeks 5–6 — Memory and Brain Fog

Primary goals:

- Clarify normal concerns, attention, fatigue, and medical boundaries
- Avoid diagnosing through checklists

Possible assets:

- Is brain fog a diagnosis?
- Memory versus attention
- When persistent cognitive concerns need evaluation
- Carousel and Short

### Weeks 7–8 — Tremor and Parkinson’s Education

Primary goals:

- Clarify tremor differential
- Introduce Parkinson’s education without fear

Possible assets:

- Not every tremor is Parkinson’s disease
- Parkinson’s motor and non-motor symptoms

2. Stop paid promotion if necessary
3. Correct website copy
4. Correct Naver adaptation
5. Correct social captions and visuals
6. Correct YouTube title, description, captions, or video
7. Correct newsletters
8. Correct product pages
9. Notify collaborators
10. Record completion

The correction is incomplete until material derivatives are addressed.

## 50. Hold and Cancellation

Place an item on Hold when:

- Evidence is insufficient
- Reviewer capacity is unavailable
- Product strategy is unresolved
- Regulatory classification is unresolved
- Formula is changing
- Rights are unresolved
- The topic duplicates a better source
- The planned format cannot preserve nuance
- The content is no longer strategically important

Cancel when:

- The topic is unsafe
- The topic requires misleading framing
- The topic is outside scope
- The content would damage trust
- A better canonical asset already exists
- Maintenance burden is unacceptable

## 55. Final Calendar Principles

The calendar is not a quota.

A blank slot is not a failure.

A delayed high-quality article is preferable to an unsafe rushed article.

Every short-form asset should connect to a responsible source.

Every product-related asset should have a legitimate educational context.

Every research post should preserve uncertainty.

Every symptom post should preserve medical boundaries.

Every platform should serve a defined role.

The calendar should not ask:

> “What can we post today?”

It should ask:

> “What does the audience need to understand next, and what is the safest and most useful format for that lesson?”

NervLock should publish deliberately, review rigorously, update transparently, and allow the content system to grow only as fast as its quality can be maintained.
