## 1. Purpose

This document records material NervLock decisions.

Its purpose is to ensure that approved decisions are:

- Easy to locate
- Clearly dated
- Linked to their rationale
- Assigned to a decision owner
- Distinguished from proposals and working assumptions
- Protected from silent reinterpretation
- Communicated consistently to ChatGPT, Claude, Claude Code, designers, editors, manufacturers, partners, and human reviewers
- Traceable when a later revision is proposed
- Preserved when people, tools, or workflows change

This document prevents the organization from repeatedly reopening settled issues without a material reason.

It also prevents an implementation partner from treating a suggestion, draft, or outdated conversation as an approved decision.

---

## 2. Governing Decision Principle

NervLock follows one central decision rule:

> A material decision must be explicit, attributable, versioned, and reversible only through a new documented decision.

A decision is not established merely because:

- It appeared in an AI response
- It was discussed in a chat
- It was implemented in code
- It appeared in a draft
- A competitor uses the same approach
- A supplier recommended it
- A team member assumed it
- It was present in an older file

Only a clearly recorded decision with an authorized status should guide irreversible work.

---

## 3. Authority and Hierarchy

Decision authority follows this order:

1. Applicable law and official regulatory requirements
2. Immediate public and patient safety
3. Authorized human decision
4. Approved core governance documents
5. Approved dynamic working documents
6. Approved task-specific implementation instructions
7. Implementation preferences
8. AI suggestions

A decision in this log must not override:

- Current law
- Medical safety
- Verified evidence
- Regulatory requirements
- Reference integrity

When a decision becomes inconsistent with a higher-priority requirement, it must be reviewed and superseded.

## 5. What Does Not Need a Formal Decision Entry

Routine actions generally do not require a decision entry unless they affect strategy or risk.

Examples:

- Correcting a spelling error
- Moving a file without changing authority
- Scheduling an already approved post
- Exporting an approved image format
- Minor CSS adjustment
- Routine status update
- Repeating an approved workflow

Material revisions should be recorded in `W05_Revision_Log.md`.

## 6. Decision Statuses

Use only the following statuses.

### Approved

The decision is authorized and active.

### Approved With Conditions

The decision is active only within documented conditions.

### Working Position

The current preferred direction, but not final.

### Pending Human Decision

Options have been evaluated, but final human approval is missing.

### Hold

No further implementation should proceed until a stated condition is resolved.

### Rejected

The option was considered and not approved.

### Superseded

A newer decision has replaced the decision.

### Withdrawn

The decision is no longer active and has no replacement yet.

### Emergency Interim Decision

A temporary safety or operational decision pending full review.

Do not use ambiguous labels such as:

- Probably approved
- Almost final
- Tentative approval
- Good enough
- Assume approved

# PART XI — DECISION IMPLEMENTATION CONTROL

## 62. Implementation Rule

A decision is not operationally complete until:

- The affected documents are updated
- The implementation owner is identified
- The affected channels are identified
- The technical or editorial work is completed
- QA is completed
- The final version is verified
- The decision status is updated if necessary

Implementation must not silently modify the decision.

---

## 63. Decision-to-Task Conversion

For each implementation decision, create a task containing:

- Decision ID
- Objective
- Governing documents
- Current system to inspect
- Protected elements
- Files affected
- Required changes
- Prohibited changes
- Tests
- Rollback
- Deliverables
- Approval gate

Claude and Claude Code should receive the decision ID in their task instructions.

---

## 64. Decision Conflict Review

When a proposed task conflicts with a logged decision:

1. Stop implementation
2. Identify the conflicting decision
3. Determine whether new evidence, law, safety, or strategy justifies reconsideration
4. Prepare a decision-revision memo
5. Obtain human approval
6. Mark the old decision Superseded if changed
7. Update affected documents
8. Record the revision in W05

Do not silently override an approved decision.

---

## 65. Supersession Rule

A new decision that replaces an old decision must state:

- Old decision ID
- New decision ID
- What changed
- Why
- Effective date
- Transition plan
- Affected assets
- Whether old content must be corrected or withdrawn

The old decision remains in the log with status:

`Superseded`

- Safety issue

- Dangerous medical error
- Regulatory notice
- Platform suspension

It must include:

- Immediate action
- Risk
- Owner
- Expiration or review time
- Full-review requirement

Emergency status does not become permanent silently.
