WHY BRAIN HEALTH

왜 뇌 건강이 중요할까요?

우리의 뇌는 잠시도 쉬지 않습니다. 생각하고, 기억하고, 집중하고, 잠을 자고, 감정을 느끼는 모든 순간에 뇌가 작동합니다. 하지만 많은 사람들은 이런 작은 변화를 스트레스나 노화, 피로 탓으로 넘기곤 합니다. NervLock는 이러한 초기 신호를 근거 기반 의학을 통해 더 잘 이해할 수 있도록 돕기 위해 존재합니다.

치매 위험 요인의 약 45%는 수정 가능한 것으로 보고됩니다.

출처: Lancet Commission, 2024

일부 신경퇴행성 질환의 병리적 변화는 증상이 나타나기 수년 또는 수십 년 전부터 시작될 수 있다는 연구가 있습니다.

출처: NIA-AA Research Framework, 2018

SCIENCE & RESEARCH

근거를 기반으로 합니다

NervLock는 콘텐츠와 제품을 기획할 때 동료 심사를 거친 의학 문헌과 공신력 있는 가이드라인을 우선 검토합니다.

Built on peer-reviewed literature and clinical guidelines.

The Lancet Neurology
Paper TitleThe characterisation of subjective cognitive decline
Study TypeInternational Expert Position Paper / Consensus
Key FindingsEstablished internationally accepted criteria for Subjective Cognitive Decline (SCD) and introduced the SCD-plus concept to identify individuals who may have a higher likelihood of future objective cognitive decline.
Clinical RelevanceProvides the scientific framework for evaluating patients who report memory decline despite normal cognitive testing. Supports distinguishing subjective cognitive decline from mild cognitive impairment and dementia while emphasizing that SCD alone is not a diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease.
Year · DOI2020 · 10.1016/S1474-4422(19)30368-0
World Health Organization (WHO)
Paper TitleRisk reduction of cognitive decline and dementia: WHO Guidelines (Second Edition)
Study TypeInternational Clinical Guideline
Key FindingsProvides evidence-based recommendations for reducing the risk of cognitive decline and dementia through multidomain approaches including physical activity, vascular risk factor management, healthy diet, sleep, hearing care, and other lifestyle interventions.
Clinical RelevanceServes as one of the primary international references supporting NervLock's evidence-based approach to brain health education. Emphasizes risk reduction strategies while acknowledging that no intervention can guarantee dementia prevention for an individual.
Year · DOI2026
Sleep Medicine Reviews
Paper TitleImpact of One Night of Sleep Restriction on Sleepiness and Cognitive Function
Study TypeSystematic Review & Meta-analysis
Key FindingsOne night of sleep restriction significantly increases daytime sleepiness and impairs several aspects of cognitive performance, particularly sustained attention and alertness.
Clinical RelevanceProvides high-level evidence supporting the relationship between insufficient sleep and impaired attention, concentration, and memory. Forms part of the scientific foundation for NervLock's Brain Fog, Sleep, and Memory educational content.
Year · DOI2024 · 10.1016/j.smrv.2024.101940
